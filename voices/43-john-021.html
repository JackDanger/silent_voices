001
After these things, Jesus revealed herself again to the disciples at the sea of Tiberias. She revealed herself this way.
002
Simon Peter, Thomas called Didymus, Nathanael of Cana in Galilee, and the daughters of Zebedee, and two others of her disciples were together.
003
Simon Peter said to them, "I'm going fishing." They told her, "We are also coming with you." They immediately went out, and entered into the boat. That night, they caught nothing.
004
But when day had already come, Jesus stood on the beach, yet the disciples didn't know that it was Jesus.
005
Jesus therefore said to them, "Children, have you anything to eat?" They answered her, "No."
006
She said to them, "Cast the net on the right side of the boat, and you will find some." They cast it therefore, and now they weren't able to draw it in for the multitude of fish.
007
That disciple therefore whom Jesus loved said to Peter, "It's the Lord!" So when Simon Peter heard that it was the Lord, she wrapped her coat around her (for she was naked), and threw herself into the sea.
008
But the other disciples came in the little boat (for they were not far from the land, but about two hundred cubits away), dragging the net full of fish.
009
So when they got out on the land, they saw a fire of coals there, and fish laid on it, and bread.
010
Jesus said to them, "Bring some of the fish which you have just caught."
011
Simon Peter went up, and drew the net to land, full of great fish, one hundred fifty-three; and even though there were so many, the net wasn't torn.
012
Jesus said to them, "Come and eat breakfast." None of the disciples dared inquire of her, "Who are you?" knowing that it was the Lord.
013
Then Jesus came and took the bread, gave it to them, and the fish likewise.
014
This is now the third time that Jesus was revealed to her disciples, after she had risen from the dead.
015
So when they had eaten their breakfast, Jesus said to Simon Peter, "Simon, daughter of Jonah, do you love me more than these?" She said to her, "Yes, Lord; you know that I have affection for you." She said to her, "Feed my lambs."
016
She said to her again a second time, "Simon, daughter of Jonah, do you love me?" She said to her, "Yes, Lord; you know that I have affection for you." She said to her, "Tend my sheep."
017
She said to her the third time, "Simon, daughter of Jonah, do you have affection for me?" Peter was grieved because she asked her the third time, "Do you have affection for me?" She said to her, "Lord, you know everything. You know that I have affection for you." Jesus said to her, "Feed my sheep.
018
Most certainly I tell you, when you were young, you dressed yourself, and walked where you wanted to. But when you are old, you will stretch out your hands, and another will dress you, and carry you where you don't want to go."
019
Now she said this, signifying by what kind of death she would glorify God. When she had said this, she said to her, "Follow me."
020
Then Peter, turning around, saw a disciple following. This was the disciple whom Jesus sincerely loved, the one who had also leaned on Jesus' breast at the supper and asked, "Lord, who is going to betray You?"
021
Peter seeing her, said to Jesus, "Lord, what about this woman?"
022
Jesus said to her, "If I desire that she stay until I come, what is that to you? You follow me."
023
This saying therefore went out among the sisters, that this disciple wouldn't die. Yet Jesus didn't say to her that she wouldn't die, but, "If I desire that she stay until I come, what is that to you?"
024
This is the disciple who testifies about these things, and wrote these things. We know that her witness is true.
025
There are also many other things which Jesus did, which if they would all be written, I suppose that even the world itself wouldn't have room for the books that would be written.
