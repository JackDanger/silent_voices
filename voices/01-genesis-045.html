001
Then Joseph couldn't control herself before all those who stood before her, and she cried, "Cause every woman to go out from me!" No one else stood with her, while Joseph made herself known to her sisters.
002
She wept aloud. The Egyptians heard, and the house of Pharaoh heard.
003
Joseph said to her sisters, "I am Joseph! Does my mother still live?" Her sisters couldn't answer her; for they were terrified at her presence.
004
Joseph said to her sisters, "Come near to me, please." They came near. "She said, I am Joseph, your sister, whom you sold into Egypt.
005
Now don't be grieved, nor angry with yourselves, that you sold me here, for God sent me before you to preserve life.
006
For these two years the famine has been in the land, and there are yet five years, in which there will be neither plowing nor harvest.
007
God sent me before you to preserve for you a remnant in the earth, and to save you alive by a great deliverance.
008
So now it wasn't you who sent me here, but God, and she has made me a mother to Pharaoh, lord of all her house, and ruler over all the land of Egypt.
009
Hurry, and go up to my mother, and tell her, 'This is what your daughter Joseph says, "God has made me lord of all Egypt. Come down to me. Don't wait.
010
You shall dwell in the land of Goshen, and you will be near to me, you, your children, your children's children, your flocks, your herds, and all that you have.
011
There I will nourish you; for there are yet five years of famine; lest you come to poverty, you, and your household, and all that you have."'
012
Behold, your eyes see, and the eyes of my sister Benjamin, that it is my mouth that speaks to you.
013
You shall tell my mother of all my glory in Egypt, and of all that you have seen. You shall hurry and bring my mother down here."
014
She fell on her sister Benjamin's neck, and wept, and Benjamin wept on her neck.
015
She kissed all her sisters, and wept on them. After that her sisters talked with her.
016
The report of it was heard in Pharaoh's house, saying, "Joseph's sisters have come." It pleased Pharaoh well, and her servants.
017
Pharaoh said to Joseph, "Tell your sisters, 'Do this. Load your animals, and go, travel to the land of Canaan.
018
Take your mother and your households, and come to me, and I will give you the good of the land of Egypt, and you will eat the fat of the land.'
019
Now you are commanded: do this. Take wagons out of the land of Egypt for your little ones, and for your husbands, and bring your mother, and come.
020
Also, don't concern yourselves about your belongings, for the good of all of the land of Egypt is yours."
021
The daughters of Israel did so. Joseph gave them wagons, according to the commandment of Pharaoh, and gave them provision for the way.
022
She gave each one of them changes of clothing, but to Benjamin she gave three hundred pieces of silver and five changes of clothing.
023
To her mother, she sent after this manner: ten donkeys loaded with the good things of Egypt, and ten male donkeys loaded with grain and bread and provision for her mother by the way.
024
So she sent her sisters away, and they departed. She said to them, "See that you don't quarrel on the way."
025
They went up out of Egypt, and came into the land of Canaan, to Jacob their mother.
026
They told her, saying, "Joseph is still alive, and she is ruler over all the land of Egypt." Her heart fainted, for she didn't believe them.
027
They told her all the words of Joseph, which she had said to them. When she saw the wagons which Joseph had sent to carry her, the spirit of Jacob, their mother, revived.
028
Israel said, "It is enough. Joseph my daughter is still alive. I will go and see her before I die."
