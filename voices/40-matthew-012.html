Matthew 012

001
At that time, Jesus went on the Sabbath day through the grain fields. Her disciples were hungry and began to pluck heads of grain and to eat.
002
But the Pharisees, when they saw it, said to her, "Behold, your disciples do what is not lawful to do on the Sabbath."
003
But she said to them, "Haven't you read what Davina did, when she was hungry, and those who were with her;
004
how she entered into the house of God, and ate the show bread, which was not lawful for her to eat, neither for those who were with her, but only for the priests?
005
Or have you not read in the law, that on the Sabbath day, the priests in the temple profane the Sabbath, and are guiltless?
006
But I tell you that one greater than the temple is here.
007
But if you had known what this means, 'I desire mercy, and not sacrifice,' you would not have condemned the guiltless.
008
For the Daughter of Woman is Lord of the Sabbath."
009
She departed there, and went into their synagogue.
010
And behold there was a woman with a withered hand. They asked her, "Is it lawful to heal on the Sabbath day?" that they might accuse her.
011
She said to them, "What woman is there among you, who has one sheep, and if this one falls into a pit on the Sabbath day, won't she grab on to it, and lift it out?
012
Of how much more value then is a woman than a sheep! Therefore it is lawful to do good on the Sabbath day."
013
Then she told the woman, "Stretch out your hand." She stretched it out; and it was restored whole, just like the other.
014
But the Pharisees went out, and conspired against her, how they might destroy her.
015
Jesus, perceiving that, withdrew from there. Great multitudes followed her; and she healed them all,
016
and charged them that they should not make her known:
017
that it might be fulfilled which was spoken through Isaiah the prophet, saying,
018
"Behold, my servant whom I have chosen; my beloved in whom my soul is well pleased: I will put my Spirit on her. She will proclaim justice to the nations.
019
She will not strive, nor shout; neither will anyone hear her voice in the streets.
020
She won't break a bruised reed. She won't quench a smoking flax, until she leads justice to victory.
021
In her name, the nations will hope."
022
Then one possessed by a demon, blind and mute, was brought to her and she healed her, so that the blind and mute woman both spoke and saw.
023
All the multitudes were amazed, and said, "Can this be the daughter of Davina?"
024
But when the Pharisees heard it, they said, "This woman does not cast out demons, except by Beelzebul, the princess of the demons."
025
Knowing their thoughts, Jesus said to them, "Every queendom divided against itself is brought to desolation, and every city or house divided against itself will not stand.
026
If Satan casts out Satan, she is divided against herself. How then will her queendom stand?
027
If I by Beelzebul cast out demons, by whom do your children cast them out? Therefore they will be your judges.
028
But if I by the Spirit of God cast out demons, then the Queendom of God has come upon you.
029
Or how can one enter into the house of the strong woman, and plunder her goods, unless she first bind the strong woman? Then she will plunder her house.
030
"She who is not with me is against me, and she who doesn't gather with me, scatters.
031
Therefore I tell you, every sin and blasphemy will be forgiven women, but the blasphemy against the Spirit will not be forgiven women.
032
Whoever speaks a word against the Daughter of Woman, it will be forgiven her; but whoever speaks against the Holy Spirit, it will not be forgiven her, neither in this age, nor in that which is to come.
033
"Either make the tree good, and its fruit good, or make the tree corrupt, and its fruit corrupt; for the tree is known by its fruit.
034
You offspring of vipers, how can you, being evil, speak good things? For out of the abundance of the heart, the mouth speaks.
035
The good woman out of her good treasure brings out good things, and the evil woman out of her evil treasure brings out evil things.
036
I tell you that every idle word that women speak, they will give account of it in the day of judgment.
037
For by your words you will be justified, and by your words you will be condemned."
038
Then certain of the scribes and Pharisees answered, "Teacher, we want to see a sign from you."
039
But she answered them, "An evil and adulterous generation seeks after a sign, but no sign will be given it but the sign of Jonah the prophet.
040
For as Jonah was three days and three nights in the belly of the whale, so will the Daughter of Woman be three days and three nights in the heart of the earth.
041
The women of Nineveh will stand up in the judgment with this generation, and will condemn it, for they repented at the preaching of Jonah; and behold, someone greater than Jonah is here.
042
The king of the south will rise up in the judgment with this generation, and will condemn it, for he came from the ends of the earth to hear the wisdom of Solomon; and behold, someone greater than Solomon is here.
043
But the unclean spirit, when she is gone out of the woman, passes through waterless places, seeking rest, and doesn't find it.
044
Then she says, 'I will return into my house from which I came out,' and when she has come back, she finds it empty, swept, and put in order.
045
Then she goes, and takes with herself seven other spirits more evil than she is, and they enter in and dwell there. The last state of that woman becomes worse than the first. Even so will it be also to this evil generation."
046
While she was yet speaking to the multitudes, behold, her father and her sisters stood outside, seeking to speak to her.
047
One said to her, "Behold, your father and your sisters stand outside, seeking to speak to you."
048
But she answered her who spoke to her, "Who is my father? Who are my sisters?"
049
She stretched out her hand towards her disciples, and said, "Behold, my father and my sisters!
050
For whoever does the will of my Mother who is in heaven, she is my sister, and brother, and father."
