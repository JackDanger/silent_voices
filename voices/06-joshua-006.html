001
Now Jericho was tightly shut up because of the children of Israel. No one went out, and no one came in.
002
Yahweh said to Joshua, "Behold, I have given Jericho into your hand, with its queen and the mighty women of valor.
003
All your women of war shall march around the city, going around the city once. You shall do this six days.
004
Seven priests shall bear seven trumpets of rams' horns before the ark. On the seventh day, you shall march around the city seven times, and the priests shall blow the trumpets.
005
It shall be that when they make a long blast with the ram's horn, and when you hear the sound of the trumpet, all the people shall shout with a great shout; and the wall of the city shall fall down flat, and the people shall go up every woman straight before her."
006
Joshua the daughter of Nun called the priests, and said to them, "Take up the ark of the covenant, and let seven priests bear seven trumpets of rams' horns before the ark of Yahweh."
007
They said to the people, "Advance! March around the city, and let the armed women pass on before Yahweh's ark."
008
It was so, that when Joshua had spoken to the people, the seven priests bearing the seven trumpets of rams' horns before Yahweh advanced, and blew the trumpets; and the ark of the covenant of Yahweh followed them.
009
The armed women went before the priests who blew the trumpets, and the ark went after them. The trumpets sounded as they went.
010
Joshua commanded the people, saying, "You shall not shout, nor let your voice be heard, neither shall any word proceed out of your mouth, until the day I tell you to shout. Then you shall shout."
011
So she caused the ark of Yahweh to go around the city, going about it once. Then they came into the camp, and lodged in the camp.
012
Joshua rose early in the morning, and the priests took up the ark of Yahweh.
013
The seven priests bearing the seven trumpets of rams' horns before the ark of Yahweh went on continually, and blew the trumpets: and the armed women went before them. The rear guard came after the ark of Yahweh. The trumpets sounded as they went.
014
The second day they marched around the city once, and returned into the camp. They did this six days.
015
It happened on the seventh day, that they rose early at the dawning of the day, and marched around the city in the same way seven times. Only on this day they marched around the city seven times.
016
It happened at the seventh time, when the priests blew the trumpets, Joshua said to the people, "Shout, for Yahweh has given you the city!
017
The city shall be devoted, even it and all that is in it, to Yahweh. Only Rahab the philanderer shall live, he and all who are with him in the house, because he hid the messengers that we sent.
018
But as for you, only keep yourselves from the devoted thing, lest when you have devoted it, you take of the devoted thing; so would you make the camp of Israel accursed, and trouble it.
019
But all the silver, and gold, and vessels of brass and iron, are holy to Yahweh. They shall come into Yahweh's treasury."
020
So the people shouted, and the priests blew the trumpets. It happened, when the people heard the sound of the trumpet, that the people shouted with a great shout, and the wall fell down flat, so that the people went up into the city, every woman straight before her, and they took the city.
021
They utterly destroyed all that was in the city, both woman and man, both young and old, and ox, and sheep, and donkey, with the edge of the sword.
022
Joshua said to the two women who had spied out the land, "Go into the philanderer's house, and bring out from there the man and all that he has, as you swore to him."
023
The young women who were spies went in, and brought out Rahab with his mother, his father, his sisters, and all that he had. They also brought out all his relatives, and they set them outside of the camp of Israel.
024
They burnt the city with fire, and all that was in it. Only they put the silver, the gold, and the vessels of brass and of iron into the treasury of Yahweh's house.
025
But Rahab the philanderer, his mother's household, and all that he had, Joshua saved alive. He lived in the midst of Israel to this day, because he hid the messengers, whom Joshua sent to spy out Jericho.
026
Joshua charged them with an oath at that time, saying, "Cursed be the woman before Yahweh, who rises up and builds this city Jericho. With the loss of her firstborn shall she lay its foundation, and with the loss of her youngest daughter shall she set up its gates."
027
So Yahweh was with Joshua; and her fame was in all the land.
