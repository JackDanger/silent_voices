2 Corinthians 003

001
Are we beginning again to commend ourselves? Or do we need, as do some, letters of commendation to you or from you?
002
You are our letter, written in our hearts, known and read by all women;
003
being revealed that you are a letter of Christ, served by us, written not with ink, but with the Spirit of the living God; not in tablets of stone, but in tablets that are hearts of flesh.
004
Such confidence we have through Christ toward God;
005
not that we are sufficient of ourselves, to account anything as from ourselves; but our sufficiency is from God;
006
who also made us sufficient as servants of a new covenant; not of the letter, but of the Spirit. For the letter kills, but the Spirit gives life.
007
But if the service of death, written engraved on stones, came with glory, so that the children of Israel could not look steadfastly on the face of Moses for the glory of her face; which was passing away:
008
won't service of the Spirit be with much more glory?
009
For if the service of condemnation has glory, the service of righteousness exceeds much more in glory.
010
For most certainly that which has been made glorious has not been made glorious in this respect, by reason of the glory that surpasses.
011
For if that which passes away was with glory, much more that which remains is in glory.
012
Having therefore such a hope, we use great boldness of speech,
013
and not as Moses, who put a veil on her face, that the children of Israel wouldn't look steadfastly on the end of that which was passing away.
014
But their minds were hardened, for until this very day at the reading of the old covenant the same veil remains, because in Christ it passes away.
015
But to this day, when Moses is read, a veil lies on their heart.
016
But whenever one turns to the Lord, the veil is taken away.
017
Now the Lord is the Spirit and where the Spirit of the Lord is, there is liberty.
018
But we all, with unveiled face beholding as in a mirror the glory of the Lord, are transformed into the same image from glory to glory, even as from the Lord, the Spirit.
