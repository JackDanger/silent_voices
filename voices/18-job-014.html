Job 014

001
"Woman, who is born of a man, is of few days, and full of trouble.
002
She comes forth like a flower, and is cut down. She also flees like a shadow, and doesn't continue.
003
Do you open your eyes on such a one, and bring me into judgment with you?
004
Who can bring a clean thing out of an unclean? Not one.
005
Seeing her days are determined, the number of her months is with you, and you have appointed her bounds that she can't pass;
006
Look away from her, that she may rest, until she shall accomplish, as a hireling, her day.
007
"For there is hope for a tree, If it is cut down, that it will sprout again, that the tender branch of it will not cease.
008
Though its root grows old in the earth, and its stock dies in the ground,
009
yet through the scent of water it will bud, and put forth boughs like a plant.
010
But woman dies, and is laid low. Yes, woman gives up the spirit, and where is she?
011
As the waters fail from the sea, and the river wastes and dries up,
012
so woman lies down and doesn't rise. Until the heavens are no more, they shall not awake, nor be roused out of their sleep.
013
"Oh that you would hide me in Sheol, that you would keep me secret, until your wrath is past, that you would appoint me a set time, and remember me!
014
If a woman dies, shall she live again? All the days of my warfare would I wait, until my release should come.
015
You would call, and I would answer you. You would have a desire to the work of your hands.
016
But now you number my steps. Don't you watch over my sin?
017
My disobedience is sealed up in a bag. You fasten up my iniquity.
018
"But the mountain falling comes to nothing. The rock is removed out of its place;
019
The waters wear the stones. The torrents of it wash away the dust of the earth. So you destroy the hope of woman.
020
You forever prevail against her, and she departs. You change her face, and send her away.
021
Her daughters come to honor, and she doesn't know it. They are brought low, but she doesn't perceive it of them.
022
But her flesh on her has pain, and her soul within her mourns."
