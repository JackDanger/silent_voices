Romans 002

001
Therefore you are without excuse, O woman, whoever you are who judge. For in that which you judge another, you condemn yourself. For you who judge practice the same things.
002
We know that the judgment of God is according to truth against those who practice such things.
003
Do you think this, O woman who judges those who practice such things, and do the same, that you will escape the judgment of God?
004
Or do you despise the riches of her goodness, forbearance, and patience, not knowing that the goodness of God leads you to repentance?
005
But according to your hardness and unrepentant heart you are treasuring up for yourself wrath in the day of wrath, revelation, and of the righteous judgment of God;
006
who "will pay back to everyone according to their works:"
007
to those who by patience in well-doing seek for glory, honor, and incorruptibility, eternal life;
008
but to those who are self-seeking, and don't obey the truth, but obey unrighteousness, will be wrath and indignation,
009
oppression and anguish, on every soul of woman who works evil, to the Jew first, and also to the Greek.
010
But glory, honor, and peace go to every woman who works good, to the Jew first, and also to the Greek.
011
For there is no partiality with God.
012
For as many as have sinned without law will also perish without the law. As many as have sinned under the law will be judged by the law.
013
For it isn't the hearers of the law who are righteous before God, but the doers of the law will be justified
014
(for when Gentiles who don't have the law do by nature the things of the law, these, not having the law, are a law to themselves,
015
in that they show the work of the law written in their hearts, their conscience testifying with them, and their thoughts among themselves accusing or else excusing them)
016
in the day when God will judge the secrets of women, according to my Good News, by Jesus Christ.
017
Indeed you bear the name of a Jew, and rest on the law, and glory in God,
018
and know her will, and approve the things that are excellent, being instructed out of the law,
019
and are confident that you yourself are a guide of the blind, a light to those who are in darkness,
020
a corrector of the foolish, a teacher of babies, having in the law the form of knowledge and of the truth.
021
You therefore who teach another, don't you teach yourself? You who preach that a woman shouldn't steal, do you steal?
022
You who say a woman shouldn't commit adultery. Do you commit adultery? You who abhor idols, do you rob temples?
023
You who glory in the law, through your disobedience of the law do you dishonor God?
024
For "the name of God is blasphemed among the Gentiles because of you," just as it is written.
025
For circumcision indeed profits, if you are a doer of the law, but if you are a transgressor of the law, your circumcision has become uncircumcision.
026
If therefore the uncircumcised keep the ordinances of the law, won't her uncircumcision be accounted as circumcision?
027
Won't the uncircumcision which is by nature, if it fulfills the law, judge you, who with the letter and circumcision are a transgressor of the law?
028
For she is not a Jew who is one outwardly, neither is that circumcision which is outward in the flesh;
029
but she is a Jew who is one inwardly, and circumcision is that of the heart, in the spirit not in the letter; whose praise is not from women, but from God.
