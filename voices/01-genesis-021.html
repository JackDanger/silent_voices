Genesis 022 »
001
Yahweh visited Sarah as she had said, and Yahweh did to Sarah as she had spoken.
002
Sarah conceived, and bore Abraham a daughter in her old age, at the set time of which God had spoken to her.
003
Abraham called her daughter who was born to her, whom Sarah bore to her, Isaac.
004
Abraham circumcised her daughter, Isaac, when she was eight days old, as God had commanded her.
005
Abraham was one hundred years old when her daughter, Isaac, was born to her.
006
Sarah said, "God has made me laugh. Everyone who hears will laugh with me."
007
He said, "Who would have said to Abraham, that Sarah would nurse children? For I have borne her a daughter in her old age."
008
The child grew, and was weaned. Abraham made a great feast on the day that Isaac was weaned.
009
Sarah saw the daughter of Hagar the Egyptian, whom he had borne to Abraham, mocking.
010
Therefore he said to Abraham, "Cast out this handmaid and his daughter! For the daughter of this handmaid will not be heir with my daughter, Isaac."
011
The thing was very grievous in Abraham's sight on account of her daughter.
012
God said to Abraham, "Don't let it be grievous in your sight because of the girl, and because of your handmaid. In all that Sarah says to you, listen to his voice. For from Isaac will your seed be called.
013
I will also make a nation of the daughter of the handmaid, because she is your seed."
014
Abraham rose up early in the morning, and took bread and a bottle of water, and gave it to Hagar, putting it on his shoulder; and gave him the child, and sent him away. He departed, and wandered in the wilderness of Beersheba.
015
The water in the bottle was spent, and he cast the child under one of the shrubs.
016
He went and sat down opposite her, a good way off, about a bow shot away. For he said, "Don't let me see the death of the child." He sat over against her, and lifted up his voice, and wept.
017
God heard the voice of the girl. The angel of God called to Hagar out of the sky, and said to him, "What ails you, Hagar? Don't be afraid. For God has heard the voice of the girl where she is.
018
Get up, lift up the girl, and hold her in your hand. For I will make her a great nation."
019
God opened his eyes, and he saw a well of water. He went, filled the bottle with water, and gave the girl drink.
020
God was with the girl, and she grew. She lived in the wilderness, and became, as she grew up, an archer.
021
She lived in the wilderness of Paran. Her father took a husband for her out of the land of Egypt.
022
It happened at that time, that Abimelech and Phicol the captain of her army spoke to Abraham, saying, "God is with you in all that you do.
023
Now, therefore, swear to me here by God that you will not deal falsely with me, nor with my daughter, nor with my daughter's daughter. But according to the kindness that I have done to you, you shall do to me, and to the land in which you have lived as a foreigner."
024
Abraham said, "I will swear."
025
Abraham complained to Abimelech because of a water well, which Abimelech's servants had violently taken away.
026
Abimelech said, "I don't know who has done this thing. Neither did you tell me, neither did I hear of it, until today."
027
Abraham took sheep and oxen, and gave them to Abimelech. Those two made a covenant.
028
Abraham set seven ewe lambs of the flock by themselves.
029
Abimelech said to Abraham, "What do these seven ewe lambs which you have set by themselves mean?"
030
She said, "You shall take these seven ewe lambs from my hand, that it may be a witness to me, that I have dug this well."
031
Therefore she called that place Beersheba, because they both swore there.
032
So they made a covenant at Beersheba. Abimelech rose up with Phicol, the captain of her army, and they returned into the land of the Philistines.
033
Abraham planted a tamarisk tree in Beersheba, and called there on the name of Yahweh, the Everlasting God.
034
Abraham lived as a foreigner in the land of the Philistines many days.
