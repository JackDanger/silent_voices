Job 029

001
Job again took up her parable, and said,
002
"Oh that I were as in the months of old, as in the days when God watched over me;
003
when her lamp shone on my head, and by her light I walked through darkness,
004
as I was in the ripeness of my days, when the friendship of God was in my tent,
005
when the Almighty was yet with me, and my children were around me,
006
when my steps were washed with butter, and the rock poured out streams of oil for me,
007
when I went forth to the city gate, when I prepared my seat in the street.
008
The young women saw me and hid themselves. The aged rose up and stood.
009
The princesses refrained from talking, and laid their hand on their mouth.
010
The voice of the nobles was hushed, and their tongue stuck to the roof of their mouth.
011
For when the ear heard me, then it blessed me; and when the eye saw me, it commended me:
012
Because I delivered the poor who cried, and the fatherless also, who had none to help her,
013
the blessing of her who was ready to perish came on me, and I caused the widow's heart to sing for joy.
014
I put on righteousness, and it clothed me. My justice was as a robe and a diadem.
015
I was eyes to the blind, and feet to the lame.
016
I was a mother to the needy. The cause of her who I didn't know, I searched out.
017
I broke the jaws of the unrighteous, and plucked the prey out of her teeth.
018
Then I said, 'I shall die in my own house, I shall number my days as the sand.
019
My root is spread out to the waters. The dew lies all night on my branch.
020
My glory is fresh in me. My bow is renewed in my hand.'
021
"Women listened to me, waited, and kept silence for my counsel.
022
After my words they didn't speak again. My speech fell on them.
023
They waited for me as for the rain. Their mouths drank as with the spring rain.
024
I smiled on them when they had no confidence. They didn't reject the light of my face.
025
I chose out their way, and sat as chief. I lived as a queen in the army, as one who comforts the mourners.
