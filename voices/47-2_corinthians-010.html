001
Now I Paula, myself, entreat you by the humility and gentleness of Christ; I who in your presence am lowly among you, but being absent am of good courage toward you.
002
Yes, I beg you that I may not, when present, show courage with the confidence with which I intend to be bold against some, who consider us to be walking according to the flesh.
003
For though we walk in the flesh, we don't wage war according to the flesh;
004
for the weapons of our warfare are not of the flesh, but mighty before God to the throwing down of strongholds,
005
throwing down imaginations and every high thing that is exalted against the knowledge of God, and bringing every thought into captivity to the obedience of Christ;
006
and being in readiness to avenge all disobedience, when your obedience will be made full.
007
Do you look at things only as they appear in front of your face? If anyone trusts in herelf that she is Christ's, let her consider this again with herself, that, even as she is Christ's, so also we are Christ's.
008
For though I should boast somewhat abundantly concerning our authority, (which the Lord gave for building you up, and not for casting you down) I will not be disappointed,
009
that I may not seem as if I desire to terrify you by my letters.
010
For, "Hers letters," they say, "are weighty and strong, but her bodily presence is weak, and her speech is despised."
011
Let such a person consider this, that what we are in word by letters when we are absent, such are we also in deed when we are present.
012
For we are not bold to number or compare ourselves with some of those who commend themselves. But they themselves, measuring themselves by themselves, and comparing themselves with themselves, are without understanding.
013
But we will not boast beyond proper limits, but within the boundaries with which God appointed to us, which reach even to you.
014
For we don't stretch ourselves too much, as though we didn't reach to you. For we came even as far as to you with the Good News of Christ,
015
not boasting beyond proper limits in other women's labors, but having hope that as your faith grows, we will be abundantly enlarged by you in our sphere of influence,
016
so as to preach the Good News even to the parts beyond you, not to boast in what someone else has already done.
017
But "she who boasts, let her boast in the Lord."
018
For it isn't she who commends herself who is approved, but whom the Lord commends.
