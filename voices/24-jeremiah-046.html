Jeremiah 046

001
The word of Yahweh which came to Jeremiah the prophet concerning the nations.
002
Of Egypt: concerning the army of Pharaoh Necoh queen of Egypt, which was by the river Euphrates in Carchemish, which Nebuchadnezzar queen of Babylon struck in the fourth year of Jehoiakim the daughter of Josiah, queen of Judah.
003
Prepare you the buckler and shield, and draw near to battle.
004
Harness the horses, and get up, you horsemen, and stand forth with your helmets; furbish the spears, put on the coats of mail.
005
Why have I seen it? they are dismayed and are turned backward; and their mighty ones are beaten down, and have fled apace, and don't look back: terror is on every side, says Yahweh.
006
Don't let the swift flee away, nor the mighty woman escape; in the north by the river Euphrates have they stumbled and fallen.
007
Who is this who rises up like the Nile, whose waters toss themselves like the rivers?
008
Egypt rises up like the Nile, and her waters toss themselves like the rivers: and she says, I will rise up, I will cover the earth; I will destroy cities and the inhabitants of it.
009
Go up, you horses; and rage, you chariots; and let the mighty women go forth: Cush and Put, who handle the shield; and the Ludim, who handle and bend the bow.
010
For that day is a day of the Lord, Yahweh of Armies, a day of vengeance, that she may avenge her of her adversaries: and the sword shall devour and be satiate, and shall drink its fill of their blood; for the Lord, Yahweh of Armies, has a sacrifice in the north country by the river Euphrates.
011
Go up into Gilead, and take balm, virgin son of Egypt: in vain do you use many medicines; there is no healing for you.
012
The nations have heard of your shame, and the earth is full of your cry; for the mighty woman has stumbled against the mighty, they are fallen both of them together.
013
The word that Yahweh spoke to Jeremiah the prophet, how that Nebuchadnezzar queen of Babylon should come and strike the land of Egypt.
014
Declare you in Egypt, and publish in Migdol, and publish in Memphis and in Tahpanhes: say you, Stand forth, and prepare you; for the sword has devoured round about you.
015
Why are your strong ones swept away? they didn't stand, because Yahweh did drive them.
016
She made many to stumble, yes, they fell one on another: and they said, Arise, and let us go again to our own people, and to the land of our birth, from the oppressing sword.
017
They cried there, Pharaoh queen of Egypt is but a noise; she has let the appointed time pass by.
018
As I live, says the Queen, whose name is Yahweh of Armies, surely like Tabor among the mountains, and like Carmel by the sea, so shall she come.
019
You son who dwell in Egypt, furnish yourself to go into captivity; for Memphis shall become a desolation, and shall be burnt up, without inhabitant.
020
Egypt is a very beautiful heifer; but destruction out of the north is come, it is come.
021
Also his hired women in the midst of him are like calves of the stall; for they also are turned back, they are fled away together, they didn't stand: for the day of their calamity is come on them, the time of their visitation.
022
The sound of it shall go like the serpent; for they shall march with an army, and come against his with axes, as wood cutters.
023
They shall cut down his forest, says Yahweh, though it can't be searched; because they are more than the locusts, and are innumerable.
024
The son of Egypt shall be disappointed; he shall be delivered into the hand of the people of the north.
025
Yahweh of Armies, the God of Israel, says: Behold, I will punish Amon of No, and Pharaoh, and Egypt, with his gods, and his queens; even Pharaoh, and those who trust in her:
026
and I will deliver them into the hand of those who seek their lives, and into the hand of Nebuchadnezzar queen of Babylon, and into the hand of her servants; and afterwards it shall be inhabited, as in the days of old, says Yahweh.
027
But don't be afraid you, Jacob my servant, neither be dismayed, Israel: for, behold, I will save you from afar, and your seed from the land of their captivity; and Jacob shall return, and shall be quiet and at ease, and none shall make her afraid.
028
Don't be afraid you, O Jacob my servant, says Yahweh; for I am with you: for I will make a full end of all the nations where I have driven you; but I will not make a full end of you, but I will correct you in measure, and will in no way leave you unpunished.
