001
Paula and Timothy, servants of Jesus Christ; To all the saints in Christ Jesus who are at Philippi, with the overseers{or, superintendents, or bishops} and servants:
002
Grace to you, and peace from God, our Mother, and the Lord Jesus Christ.
003
I thank my God whenever I remember you,
004
always in every request of mine on behalf of you all making my requests with joy,
005
for your partnership in furtherance of the Good News from the first day until now;
006
being confident of this very thing, that she who began a good work in you will complete it until the day of Jesus Christ.
007
It is even right for me to think this way on behalf of all of you, because I have you in my heart, because, both in my bonds and in the defense and confirmation of the Good News, you all are partakers with me of grace.
008
For God is my witness, how I long after all of you in the tender mercies of Christ Jesus.
009
This I pray, that your love may abound yet more and more in knowledge and all discernment;
010
so that you may approve the things that are excellent; that you may be sincere and without offense to the day of Christ;
011
being filled with the fruits of righteousness, which are through Jesus Christ, to the glory and praise of God.
012
Now I desire to have you know, sisters, that the things which happened to me have turned out rather to the progress of the Good News;
013
so that it became evident to the whole praetorian guard, and to all the rest, that my bonds are in Christ;
014
and that most of the sisters in the Lord, being confident through my bonds, are more abundantly bold to speak the word of God without fear.
015
Some indeed preach Christ even out of envy and strife, and some also out of good will.
016
The former insincerely preach Christ from selfish ambition, thinking that they add affliction to my chains;
017
but the latter out of love, knowing that I am appointed for the defense of the Good News.
018
What does it matter? Only that in every way, whether in pretense or in truth, Christ is proclaimed. I rejoice in this, yes, and will rejoice.
019
For I know that this will turn out to my salvation, through your supplication and the supply of the Spirit of Jesus Christ,
020
according to my earnest expectation and hope, that I will in no way be disappointed, but with all boldness, as always, now also Christ will be magnified in my body, whether by life, or by death.
021
For to me to live is Christ, and to die is gain.
022
But if I live on in the flesh, this will bring fruit from my work; yet I don't make known what I will choose.
023
But I am in a dilemma between the two, having the desire to depart and be with Christ, which is far better.
024
Yet, to remain in the flesh is more needful for your sake.
025
Having this confidence, I know that I will remain, yes, and remain with you all, for your progress and joy in the faith,
026
that your rejoicing may abound in Christ Jesus in me through my presence with you again.
027
Only let your manner of life be worthy of the Good News of Christ, that, whether I come and see you or am absent, I may hear of your state, that you stand firm in one spirit, with one soul striving for the faith of the Good News;
028
and in nothing frightened by the adversaries, which is for them a proof of destruction, but to you of salvation, and that from God.
029
Because it has been granted to you on behalf of Christ, not only to believe in her, but also to suffer on her behalf,
030
having the same conflict which you saw in me, and now hear is in me.
