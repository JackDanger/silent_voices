001
Then Job answered,
002
"Oh that my anguish were weighed, and all my calamity laid in the balances!
003
For now it would be heavier than the sand of the seas, therefore have my words been rash.
004
For the arrows of the Almighty are within me. My spirit drinks up their poison. The terrors of God set themselves in array against me.
005
Does the wild donkey bray when she has grass? Or does the ox low over her fodder?
006
Can that which has no flavor be eaten without salt? Or is there any taste in the white of an egg?
007
My soul refuses to touch them. They are as loathsome food to me.
008
"Oh that I might have my request, that God would grant the thing that I long for,
009
even that it would please God to crush me; that she would let loose her hand, and cut me off!
010
Be it still my consolation, yes, let me exult in pain that doesn't spare, that I have not denied the words of the Holy One.
011
What is my strength, that I should wait? What is my end, that I should be patient?
012
Is my strength the strength of stones? Or is my flesh of brass?
013
Isn't it that I have no help in me, That wisdom is driven quite from me?
014
"To her who is ready to faint, kindness should be shown from her friend; even to her who forsakes the fear of the Almighty.
015
My sisters have dealt deceitfully as a brook, as the channel of brooks that pass away;
016
Which are black by reason of the ice, in which the snow hides itself.
017
In the dry season, they vanish. When it is hot, they are consumed out of their place.
018
The caravans that travel beside them turn aside. They go up into the waste, and perish.
019
The caravans of Tema looked. The companies of Sheba waited for them.
020
They were distressed because they were confident. They came there, and were confounded.
021
For now you are nothing. You see a terror, and are afraid.
022
Did I say, 'Give to me?' or, 'Offer a present for me from your substance?'
023
or, 'Deliver me from the adversary's hand?' or, 'Redeem me from the hand of the oppressors?'
024
"Teach me, and I will hold my peace. Cause me to understand wherein I have erred.
025
How forcible are words of uprightness! But your reproof, what does it reprove?
026
Do you intend to reprove words, seeing that the speeches of one who is desperate are as wind?
027
Yes, you would even cast lots for the fatherless, and make merchandise of your friend.
028
Now therefore be pleased to look at me, for surely I shall not lie to your face.
029
Please return. Let there be no injustice. Yes, return again. My cause is righteous.
030
Is there injustice on my tongue? Can't my taste discern mischievous things?
