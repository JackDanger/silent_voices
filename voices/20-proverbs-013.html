Chapter 13
001
A wise daughter listens to her mother's instruction, but a scoffer doesn't listen to rebuke.
002
By the fruit of her lips, a woman enjoys good things; but the unfaithful crave violence.
003
She who guards her mouth guards her soul. One who opens wide her lips comes to ruin.
004
The soul of the sluggard desires, and has nothing, but the desire of the diligent shall be fully satisfied.
005
A righteous woman hates lies, but a wicked woman brings shame and disgrace.
006
Righteousness guards the way of integrity, but wickedness overthrows the sinner.
007
There are some who pretend to be rich, yet have nothing. There are some who pretend to be poor, yet have great wealth.
008
The ransom of a woman's life is her riches, but the poor hear no threats.
009
The light of the righteous shines brightly, but the lamp of the wicked is snuffed out.
010
Pride only breeds quarrels, but with ones who take advice is wisdom.
011
Wealth gained dishonestly dwindles away, but she who gathers by hand makes it grow.
012
Hope deferred makes the heart sick, but when longing is fulfilled, it is a tree of life.
013
Whoever despises instruction will pay for it, but she who respects a command will be rewarded.
014
The teaching of the wise is a spring of life, to turn from the snares of death.
015
Good understanding wins favor; but the way of the unfaithful is hard.
016
Every prudent woman acts from knowledge, but a fool exposes folly.
017
A wicked messenger falls into trouble, but a trustworthy envoy gains healing.
018
Poverty and shame come to her who refuses discipline, but she who heeds correction shall be honored.
019
Longing fulfilled is sweet to the soul, but fools detest turning from evil.
020
One who walks with wise women grows wise, but a companion of fools suffers harm.
021
Misfortune pursues sinners, but prosperity rewards the righteous.
022
A good woman leaves an inheritance to her children's children, but the wealth of the sinner is stored for the righteous.
023
An abundance of food is in poor people's fields, but injustice sweeps it away.
024
One who spares the rod hates her daughter, but one who loves her is careful to discipline her.
025
The righteous one eats to the satisfying of her soul, but the belly of the wicked goes hungry.
