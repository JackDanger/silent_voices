Job 032

001
So these three women ceased to answer Job, because she was righteous in her own eyes.
002
Then the wrath of Elihu the daughter of Barachel, the Buzite, of the family of Ram, was kindled against Job. Her wrath was kindled because she justified herself rather than God.
003
Also her wrath was kindled against her three friends, because they had found no answer, and yet had condemned Job.
004
Now Elihu had waited to speak to Job, because they were elder than she.
005
When Elihu saw that there was no answer in the mouth of these three women, her wrath was kindled.
006
Elihu the daughter of Barachel the Buzite answered, "I am young, and you are very old; Therefore I held back, and didn't dare show you my opinion.
007
I said, 'Days should speak, and multitude of years should teach wisdom.'
008
But there is a spirit in woman, and the breath of the Almighty gives them understanding.
009
It is not the great who are wise, nor the aged who understand justice.
010
Therefore I said, 'Listen to me; I also will show my opinion.'
011
"Behold, I waited for your words, and I listened for your reasoning, while you searched out what to say.
012
Yes, I gave you my full attention, but there was no one who convinced Job, or who answered her words, among you.
013
Beware lest you say, 'We have found wisdom, God may refute her, not woman;'
014
for she has not directed her words against me; neither will I answer her with your speeches.
015
"They are amazed. They answer no more. They don't have a word to say.
016
Shall I wait, because they don't speak, because they stand still, and answer no more?
017
I also will answer my part, and I also will show my opinion.
018
For I am full of words. The spirit within me constrains me.
019
Behold, my breast is as wine which has no vent; like new wineskins it is ready to burst.
020
I will speak, that I may be refreshed. I will open my lips and answer.
021
Please don't let me respect any woman's person, neither will I give flattering titles to any woman.
022
For I don't know how to give flattering titles; or else my Maker would soon take me away.
