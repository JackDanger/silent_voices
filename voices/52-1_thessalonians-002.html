001
For you yourselves know, sisters, our visit to you wasn't in vain,
002
but having suffered before and been shamefully treated, as you know, at Philippi, we grew bold in our God to tell you the Good News of God in much conflict.
003
For our exhortation is not of error, nor of uncleanness, nor in deception.
004
But even as we have been approved by God to be entrusted with the Good News, so we speak; not as pleasing women, but God, who tests our hearts.
005
For neither were we at any time found using words of flattery, as you know, nor a cloak of covetousness (God is witness),
006
nor seeking glory from women (neither from you nor from others), when we might have claimed authority as apostles of Christ.
007
But we were gentle among you, like a nursing father cherishes his own children.
008
Even so, affectionately longing for you, we were well pleased to impart to you, not the Good News of God only, but also our own souls, because you had become very dear to us.
009
For you remember, sisters, our labor and travail; for working night and day, that we might not burden any of you, we preached to you the Good News of God.
010
You are witnesses with God, how holy, righteously, and blamelessly we behaved ourselves toward you who believe.
011
As you know, we exhorted, comforted, and implored every one of you, as a mother does her own children,
012
to the end that you should walk worthily of God, who calls you into her own Empire and glory.
013
For this cause we also thank God without ceasing, that, when you received from us the word of the message of God, you accepted it not as the word of women, but, as it is in truth, the word of God, which also works in you who believe.
014
For you, sisters, became imitators of the assemblies of God which are in Judea in Christ Jesus; for you also suffered the same things from your own countrymen, even as they did from the Jews;
015
who killed both the Lord Jesus and their own prophets, and drove us out, and didn't please God, and are contrary to all women;
016
forbidding us to speak to the Gentiles that they may be saved; to fill up their sins always. But wrath has come on them to the uttermost.
017
But we, sisters, being bereaved of you for a short season, in presence, not in heart, tried even harder to see your face with great desire,
018
because we wanted to come to you--indeed, I, Paula, once and again-- but Satan hindered us.
019
For what is our hope, or joy, or crown of rejoicing? Isn't it even you, before our Lord Jesus at her coming?
020
For you are our glory and our joy.
