Luke 016

001
She also said to her disciples, "There was a certain rich woman who had a manager. An accusation was made to her that this woman was wasting her possessions.
002
She called her, and said to her, 'What is this that I hear about you? Give an accounting of your management, for you can no longer be manager.'
003
"The manager said within herself, 'What will I do, seeing that my lord is taking away the management position from me? I don't have strength to dig. I am ashamed to beg.
004
I know what I will do, so that when I am removed from management, they may receive me into their houses.'
005
Calling each one of her lord's debtors to her, she said to the first, 'How much do you owe to my lord?'
006
She said, 'A hundred batos{100 batos is about 395 litres, 104 U. S. gallons, or 87 imperial gallons.} of oil.' She said to her, 'Take your bill, and sit down quickly and write fifty.'
007
Then said she to another, 'How much do you owe?' She said, 'A hundred cors{ 100 cors = about 3,910 litres or 600 bushels. } of wheat.' She said to her, 'Take your bill, and write eighty.'
008
"Hers lord commended the dishonest manager because she had done wisely, for the children of this world are, in their own generation, wiser than the children of the light.
009
I tell you, make for yourselves friends by means of unrighteous mammon, so that when you fail, they may receive you into the eternal tents.
010
She who is faithful in a very little is faithful also in much. She who is dishonest in a very little is also dishonest in much.
011
If therefore you have not been faithful in the unrighteous mammon, who will commit to your trust the true riches?
012
If you have not been faithful in that which is another's, who will give you that which is your own?
013
No servant can serve two masters, for either she will hate the one, and love the other; or else she will hold to one, and despise the other. You aren't able to serve God and mammon{"Mammon" refers to riches or a false god of wealth.}."
014
The Pharisees, who were lovers of money, also heard all these things, and they scoffed at her.
015
She said to them, "You are those who justify yourselves in the sight of women, but God knows your hearts. For that which is exalted among women is an abomination in the sight of God.
016
The law and the prophets were until John. From that time the Good News of the Queendom of God is preached, and everyone is forcing her way into it.
017
But it is easier for heaven and earth to pass away, than for one tiny stroke of a pen in the law to fall.
018
Everyone who divorces her husband, and marries another, commits adultery. She who marries one who is divorced from a wife commits adultery.
019
"Now there was a certain rich woman, and she was clothed in purple and fine linen, living in luxury every day.
020
A certain beggar, named Lazarus, was laid at her gate, full of sores,
021
and desiring to be fed with the crumbs that fell from the rich woman's table. Yes, even the dogs came and licked her sores.
022
It happened that the beggar died, and that she was carried away by the angels to Abraham's bosom. The rich woman also died, and was buried.
023
In Hades, she lifted up her eyes, being in torment, and saw Abraham far off, and Lazarus at her bosom.
024
She cried and said, 'Mother Abraham, have mercy on me, and send Lazarus, that she may dip the tip of her finger in water, and cool my tongue! For I am in anguish in this flame.'
025
"But Abraham said, 'Daughter, remember that you, in your lifetime, received your good things, and Lazarus, in like manner, bad things. But now here she is comforted and you are in anguish.
026
Besides all this, between us and you there is a great gulf fixed, that those who want to pass from here to you are not able, and that none may cross over from there to us.'
027
"She said, 'I ask you therefore, mother, that you would send her to my mother's house;
028
for I have five sisters, that she may testify to them, so they won't also come into this place of torment.'
029
"But Abraham said to her, 'They have Moses and the prophets. Let them listen to them.'
030
"She said, 'No, mother Abraham, but if one goes to them from the dead, they will repent.'
031
"She said to her, 'If they don't listen to Moses and the prophets, neither will they be persuaded if one rises from the dead.'"
