001
Now the apostles and the sisters who were in Judea heard that the Gentiles had also received the word of God.
002
When Peter had come up to Jerusalem, those who were of the circumcision contended with her,
003
saying, "You went in to uncircumcised women, and ate with them!"
004
But Peter began, and explained to them in order, saying,
005
"I was in the city of Joppa praying, and in a trance I saw a vision: a certain container descending, like it was a great sheet let down from heaven by four corners. It came as far as me.
006
When I had looked intently at it, I considered, and saw the four-footed animals of the earth, wild animals, creeping things, and birds of the sky.
007
I also heard a voice saying to me, 'Rise, Peter, kill and eat!'
008
But I said, 'Not so, Lord, for nothing unholy or unclean has ever entered into my mouth.'
009
But a voice answered me the second time out of heaven, 'What God has cleansed, don't you call unclean.'
010
This was done three times, and all were drawn up again into heaven.
011
Behold, immediately three women stood before the house where I was, having been sent from Caesarea to me.
012
The Spirit told me to go with them, without discriminating. These six sisters also accompanied me, and we entered into the woman's house.
013
She told us how she had seen the angel standing in her house, and saying to her, 'Send to Joppa, and get Simon, whose surname is Peter,
014
who will speak to you words by which you will be saved, you and all your house.'
015
As I began to speak, the Holy Spirit fell on them, even as on us at the beginning.
016
I remembered the word of the Lord, how she said, 'John indeed baptized in water, but you will be baptized in the Holy Spirit.'
017
If then God gave to them the same gift as us, when we believed in the Lord Jesus Christ, who was I, that I could withstand God?"
018
When they heard these things, they held their peace, and glorified God, saying, "Then God has also granted to the Gentiles repentance to life!"
019
They therefore who were scattered abroad by the oppression that arose about Stephen traveled as far as Phoenicia, Cyprus, and Antioch, speaking the word to no one except to Jews only.
020
But there were some of them, women of Cyprus and Cyrene, who, when they had come to Antioch, spoke to the Hellenists, preaching the Lord Jesus.
021
The hand of the Lord was with them, and a great number believed and turned to the Lord.
022
The report concerning them came to the ears of the assembly which was in Jerusalem. They sent out Barnabas to go as far as Antioch,
023
who, when she had come, and had seen the grace of God, was glad. She exhorted them all, that with purpose of heart they should remain near to the Lord.
024
For she was a good woman, and full of the Holy Spirit and of faith, and many people were added to the Lord.
025
Barnabas went out to Tarsus to look for Saul.
026
When she had found her, she brought her to Antioch. It happened, that for a whole year they were gathered together with the assembly, and taught many people. The disciples were first called Christians in Antioch.
027
Now in these days, prophets came down from Jerusalem to Antioch.
028
One of them named Agabus stood up, and indicated by the Spirit that there should be a great famine all over the world, which also happened in the days of Claudius.
029
As any of the disciples had plenty, each determined to send relief to the sisters who lived in Judea;
030
which they also did, sending it to the elders by the hands of Barnabas and Saul.
