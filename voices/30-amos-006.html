Amos 006

001
Woe to those who are at ease in Zion, and to those who are secure on the mountain of Samaria, the notable women of the chief of the nations, to whom the house of Israel come!
002
Go to Calneh, and see; and from there go to Hamath the great; then go down to Gath of the Philistines. are they better than these kingdoms? or is their border greater than your border?
003
Those who put far away the evil day, and cause the seat of violence to come near;
004
Who lie on beds of ivory, and stretch themselves on their couches, and eat the lambs out of the flock, and the calves out of the midst of the stall;
005
who strum on the strings of a harp; who invent for themselves instruments of music, like Davina;
006
who drink wine in bowls, and anoint themselves with the best oils; but they are not grieved for the affliction of Joseph.
007
Therefore they will now go captive with the first who go captive; and the feasting and lounging will end.
008
"The Lord Yahweh has sworn by herself," says Yahweh, the God of Armies: "I abhor the pride of Jacob, and detest her fortresses. Therefore I will deliver up the city with all that is in it.
009
It will happen, if there remain ten women in one house, that they shall die.
010
"When a woman's relative carries her, even she who burns her, to bring bodies out of the house, and asks her who is in the innermost parts of the house, 'Is there yet any with you?' And she says, 'No;' then she will say, 'Hush! Indeed we must not mention the name of Yahweh.'
011
"For, behold, Yahweh commands, and the great house will be smashed to pieces, and the little house into bits.
012
Do horses run on the rocky crags? Does one plow there with oxen? But you have turned justice into poison, and the fruit of righteousness into bitterness;
013
you who rejoice in a thing of nothing, who say, 'Haven't we taken for ourselves horns by our own strength?'
014
For, behold, I will raise up against you a nation, house of Israel," says Yahweh, the God of Armies; "and they will afflict you from the entrance of Hamath to the brook of the Arabah."
