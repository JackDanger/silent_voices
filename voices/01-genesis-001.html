Genesis 001

001
In the beginning God{After "God," the Hebrew has the two letters "Aleph Tav" (the first and last letters of the Hebrew alphabet) as a grammatical marker.} created the heavens and the earth.
002
Now the earth was formless and empty. Darkness was on the surface of the deep. God's Spirit was hovering over the surface of the waters.
003
God said, "Let there be light," and there was light.
004
God saw the light, and saw that it was good. God divided the light from the darkness.
005
God called the light Day, and the darkness she called Night. There was evening and there was morning, one day.
006
God said, "Let there be an expanse in the middle of the waters, and let it divide the waters from the waters."
007
God made the expanse, and divided the waters which were under the expanse from the waters which were above the expanse; and it was so.
008
God called the expanse sky. There was evening and there was morning, a second day.
009
God said, "Let the waters under the sky be gathered together to one place, and let the dry land appear;" and it was so.
010
God called the dry land Earth, and the gathering together of the waters she called Seas. God saw that it was good.
011
God said, "Let the earth put forth grass, herbs yielding seed, and fruit trees bearing fruit after their kind, with its seed in it, on the earth;" and it was so.
012
The earth brought forth grass, herbs yielding seed after their kind, and trees bearing fruit, with its seed in it, after their kind; and God saw that it was good.
013
There was evening and there was morning, a third day.
014
God said, "Let there be lights in the expanse of sky to divide the day from the night; and let them be for signs, and for seasons, and for days and years;
015
and let them be for lights in the expanse of sky to give light on the earth;" and it was so.
016
God made the two great lights: the greater light to rule the day, and the lesser light to rule the night. She also made the stars.
017
God set them in the expanse of sky to give light to the earth,
018
and to rule over the day and over the night, and to divide the light from the darkness. God saw that it was good.
019
There was evening and there was morning, a fourth day.
020
God said, "Let the waters swarm with swarms of living creatures, and let birds fly above the earth in the open expanse of sky."
021
God created the large sea creatures, and every living creature that moves, with which the waters swarmed, after their kind, and every winged bird after its kind. God saw that it was good.
022
God blessed them, saying, "Be fruitful, and multiply, and fill the waters in the seas, and let birds multiply on the earth."
023
There was evening and there was morning, a fifth day.
024
God said, "Let the earth bring forth living creatures after their kind, livestock, creeping things, and animals of the earth after their kind;" and it was so.
025
God made the animals of the earth after their kind, and the livestock after their kind, and everything that creeps on the ground after its kind. God saw that it was good.
026
God said, "Let us make woman in our image, after our likeness: and let them have dominion over the fish of the sea, and over the birds of the sky, and over the livestock, and over all the earth, and over every creeping thing that creeps on the earth."
027
God created woman in her own image. In God's image she created her; female and male she created them.
028
God blessed them. God said to them, "Be fruitful, multiply, fill the earth, and subdue it. Have dominion over the fish of the sea, over the birds of the sky, and over every living thing that moves on the earth."
029
God said, "Behold, I have given you every herb yielding seed, which is on the surface of all the earth, and every tree, which bears fruit yielding seed. It will be your food.
030
To every animal of the earth, and to every bird of the sky, and to everything that creeps on the earth, in which there is life, I have given every green herb for food;" and it was so.
031
God saw everything that she had made, and, behold, it was very good. There was evening and there was morning, a sixth day.
