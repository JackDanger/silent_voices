Philippians 004

001
Therefore, my sisters, beloved and longed for, my joy and crown, so stand firm in the Lord, my beloved.
002
I exhort Euodia, and I exhort Syntyche, to think the same way in the Lord.
003
Yes, I beg you also, true yokefellow, help these men, for they labored with me in the Good News, with Clement also, and the rest of my fellow workers, whose names are in the book of life.
004
Rejoice in the Lord always! Again I will say, Rejoice!
005
Let your gentleness be known to all women. The Lord is at hand.
006
In nothing be anxious, but in everything, by prayer and petition with thanksgiving, let your requests be made known to God.
007
And the peace of God, which surpasses all understanding, will guard your hearts and your thoughts in Christ Jesus.
008
Finally, sisters, whatever things are true, whatever things are honorable, whatever things are just, whatever things are pure, whatever things are lovely, whatever things are of good report; if there is any virtue, and if there is any praise, think about these things.
009
The things which you learned, received, heard, and saw in me: do these things, and the God of peace will be with you.
010
But I rejoice in the Lord greatly, that now at length you have revived your thought for me; in which you did indeed take thought, but you lacked opportunity.
011
Not that I speak in respect to lack, for I have learned in whatever state I am, to be content in it.
012
I know how to be humbled, and I know also how to abound. In everything and in all things I have learned the secret both to be filled and to be hungry, both to abound and to be in need.
013
I can do all things through Christ, who strengthens me.
014
However you did well that you shared in my affliction.
015
You yourselves also know, you Philippians, that in the beginning of the Good News, when I departed from Macedonia, no assembly shared with me in the matter of giving and receiving but you only.
016
For even in Thessalonica you sent once and again to my need.
017
Not that I seek for the gift, but I seek for the fruit that increases to your account.
018
But I have all things, and abound. I am filled, having received from Epaphroditus the things that came from you, a sweet-smelling fragrance, an acceptable and well-pleasing sacrifice to God.
019
My God will supply every need of yours according to her riches in glory in Christ Jesus.
020
Now to our God and Mother be the glory forever and ever! Amen.
021
Greet every saint in Christ Jesus. The sisters who are with me greet you.
022
All the saints greet you, especially those who are of Caesar's household.
023
The grace of the Lord Jesus Christ be with you all. Amen.
