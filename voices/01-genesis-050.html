Genesis 050

001
Joseph fell on her mother's face, wept on her, and kissed her.
002
Joseph commanded her servants, the physicians, to embalm her mother; and the physicians embalmed Israel.
003
Forty days were fulfilled for her, for that is how many the days it takes to embalm. The Egyptians wept for her for seventy days.
004
When the days of weeping for her were past, Joseph spoke to the house of Pharaoh, saying, "If now I have found favor in your eyes, please speak in the ears of Pharaoh, saying,
005
'My mother made me swear, saying, "Behold, I am dying. Bury me in my grave which I have dug for myself in the land of Canaan." Now therefore, please let me go up and bury my mother, and I will come again.'"
006
Pharaoh said, "Go up, and bury your mother, just like she made you swear."
007
Joseph went up to bury her mother; and with her went up all the servants of Pharaoh, the elders of her house, all the elders of the land of Egypt,
008
all the house of Joseph, her sisters, and her mother's house. Only their little ones, their flocks, and their herds, they left in the land of Goshen.
009
There went up with her both chariots and horsemen. It was a very great company.
010
They came to the threshing floor of Atad, which is beyond the Jordan, and there they lamented with a very great and sore lamentation. She mourned for her mother seven days.
011
When the inhabitants of the land, the Canaanites, saw the mourning in the floor of Atad, they said, "This is a grievous mourning by the Egyptians." Therefore, the name of it was called Abel Mizraim, which is beyond the Jordan.
012
Her daughters did to her just as she commanded them,
013
for her daughters carried her into the land of Canaan, and buried her in the cave of the field of Machpelah, which Abraham bought with the field, for a possession of a burial site, from Ephron the Hittite, before Mamre.
014
Joseph returned into Egypt--she, and her sisters, and all that went up with her to bury her mother, after she had buried her mother.
015
When Joseph's sisters saw that their mother was dead, they said, "It may be that Joseph will hate us, and will fully pay us back for all of the evil which we did to her."
016
They sent a message to Joseph, saying, "Your mother commanded before she died, saying,
017
'You shall tell Joseph, "Now please forgive the disobedience of your sisters, and their sin, because they did evil to you."' Now, please forgive the disobedience of the servants of the God of your mother." Joseph wept when they spoke to her.
018
Her sisters also went and fell down before her face; and they said, "Behold, we are your servants."
019
Joseph said to them, "Don't be afraid, for am I in the place of God?
020
As for you, you meant evil against me, but God meant it for good, to bring to pass, as it is this day, to save many people alive.
021
Now therefore don't be afraid. I will nourish you and your little ones." She comforted them, and spoke kindly to them.
022
Joseph lived in Egypt, she, and her mother's house. Joseph lived one hundred ten years.
023
Joseph saw Ephraim's children to the third generation. The children also of Machir, the daughter of Manasseh, were born on Joseph's knees.
024
Joseph said to her sisters, "I am dying, but God will surely visit you, and bring you up out of this land to the land which she swore to Abraham, to Isaac, and to Jacob."
025
Joseph took an oath of the children of Israel, saying, "God will surely visit you, and you shall carry up my bones from here."
026
So Joseph died, being one hundred ten years old, and they embalmed her, and she was put in a coffin in Egypt.
