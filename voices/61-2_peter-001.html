001
Simon Peter, a servant and apostle of Jesus Christ, to those who have obtained a like precious faith with us in the righteousness of our God and Savior, Jesus Christ:
002
Grace to you and peace be multiplied in the knowledge of God and of Jesus our Lord,
003
seeing that her divine power has granted to us all things that pertain to life and godliness, through the knowledge of her who called us by her own glory and virtue;
004
by which she has granted to us her precious and exceedingly great promises; that through these you may become partakers of the divine nature, having escaped from the corruption that is in the world by lust.
005
Yes, and for this very cause adding on your part all diligence, in your faith supply moral excellence; and in moral excellence, knowledge;
006
and in knowledge, self-control; and in self-control patience; and in patience godliness;
007
and in godliness brotherly affection; and in brotherly affection, love.
008
For if these things are yours and abound, they make you to be not idle nor unfruitful to the knowledge of our Lord Jesus Christ.
009
For she who lacks these things is blind, seeing only what is near, having forgotten the cleansing from her old sins.
010
Therefore, sisters, be more diligent to make your calling and election sure. For if you do these things, you will never stumble.
011
For thus you will be richly supplied with the entrance into the eternal Queendom of our Lord and Savior, Jesus Christ.
012
Therefore I will not be negligent to remind you of these things, though you know them, and are established in the present truth.
013
I think it right, as long as I am in this tent, to stir you up by reminding you;
014
knowing that the putting off of my tent comes swiftly, even as our Lord Jesus Christ made clear to me.
015
Yes, I will make every effort that you may always be able to remember these things even after my departure.
016
For we did not follow cunningly devised fables, when we made known to you the power and coming of our Lord Jesus Christ, but we were eyewitnesses of her majesty.
017
For she received from God the Mother honor and glory, when the voice came to her from the Majestic Glory, "This is my beloved Daughter, in whom I am well pleased."
018
We heard this voice come out of heaven when we were with her on the holy mountain.
019
We have the more sure word of prophecy; and you do well that you heed it, as to a lamp shining in a dark place, until the day dawns, and the morning star arises in your hearts:
020
knowing this first, that no prophecy of Scripture is of private interpretation.
021
For no prophecy ever came by the will of woman: but holy women of God spoke, being moved by the Holy Spirit.
