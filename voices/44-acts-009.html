Chapter 9
001
But Saul, still breathing threats and slaughter against the disciples of the Lord, went to the high priestess,
002
and asked for letters from her to the synagogues of Damascus, that if she found any who were of the Way, whether women or men, she might bring them bound to Jerusalem.
003
As she traveled, it happened that she got close to Damascus, and suddenly a light from the sky shone around her.
004
She fell on the earth, and heard a voice saying to her, "Saul, Saul, why do you persecute me?"
005
She said, "Who are you, Lord?" The Lord said, "I am Jesus, whom you are persecuting.
006
But rise up, and enter into the city, and you will be told what you must do."
007
The women who traveled with her stood speechless, hearing the sound, but seeing no one.
008
Saul arose from the ground, and when her eyes were opened, she saw no one. They led her by the hand, and brought her into Damascus.
009
She was without sight for three days, and neither ate nor drank.
010
Now there was a certain disciple at Damascus named Ananias. The Lord said to her in a vision, "Ananias!" She said, "Behold, it's me, Lord."
011
The Lord said to her, "Arise, and go to the street which is called Straight, and inquire in the house of Judah for one named Saul, a woman of Tarsus. For behold, she is praying,
012
and in a vision she has seen a woman named Ananias coming in, and laying her hands on her, that she might receive her sight."
013
But Ananias answered, "Lord, I have heard from many about this woman, how much evil she did to your saints at Jerusalem.
014
Here she has authority from the chief priests to bind all who call on your name."
015
But the Lord said to her, "Go your way, for she is my chosen vessel to bear my name before the nations and queens, and the children of Israel.
016
For I will show her how many things she must suffer for my name's sake."
017
Ananias departed, and entered into the house. Laying her hands on her, she said, "Sister Saul, the Lord, who appeared to you on the road by which you came, has sent me, that you may receive your sight, and be filled with the Holy Spirit."
018
Immediately something like scales fell from her eyes, and she received her sight. She arose and was baptized.
019
She took food and was strengthened. Saul stayed several days with the disciples who were at Damascus.
020
Immediately in the synagogues she proclaimed the Christ, that she is the Daughter of God.
021
All who heard her were amazed, and said, "Isn't this she who in Jerusalem made havoc of those who called on this name? And she had come here intending to bring them bound before the chief priests!"
022
But Saul increased more in strength, and confounded the Jews who lived at Damascus, proving that this is the Christ.
023
When many days were fulfilled, the Jews conspired together to kill her,
024
but their plot became known to Saul. They watched the gates both day and night that they might kill her,
025
but her disciples took her by night, and let her down through the wall, lowering her in a basket.
026
When Saul had come to Jerusalem, she tried to join herself to the disciples; but they were all afraid of her, not believing that she was a disciple.
027
But Barnabas took her, and brought her to the apostles, and declared to them how she had seen the Lord in the way, and that she had spoken to her, and how at Damascus she had preached boldly in the name of Jesus.
028
She was with them entering into Jerusalem,
029
preaching boldly in the name of the Lord. She spoke and disputed against the Hellenists, but they were seeking to kill her.
030
When the sisters knew it, they brought her down to Caesarea, and sent her off to Tarsus.
031
So the assemblies throughout all Judea and Galilee and Samaria had peace, and were built up. They were multiplied, walking in the fear of the Lord and in the comfort of the Holy Spirit.
032
It happened, as Peter went throughout all those parts, she came down also to the saints who lived at Lydda.
033
There she found a certain woman named Aeneas, who had been bedridden for eight years, because she was paralyzed.
034
Peter said to her, "Aeneas, Jesus Christ heals you. Get up and make your bed!" Immediately she arose.
035
All who lived at Lydda and in Sharon saw her, and they turned to the Lord.
036
Now there was at Joppa a certain disciple named Tabitha, which when translated, means Dorcas. This man was full of good works and acts of mercy which he did.
037
It happened in those days that he fell sick, and died. When they had washed him, they laid him in an upper chamber.
038
As Lydda was near Joppa, the disciples, hearing that Peter was there, sent two women to her, imploring her not to delay in coming to them.
039
Peter got up and went with them. When she had come, they brought her into the upper chamber. All the widows stood by her weeping, and showing the coats and garments which Dorcas had made while he was with them.
040
Peter put them all out, and kneeled down and prayed. Turning to the body, she said, "Tabitha, get up!" He opened his eyes, and when he saw Peter, he sat up.
041
She gave him her hand, and raised him up. Calling the saints and widows, she presented him alive.
042
And it became known throughout all Joppa, and many believed in the Lord.
043
It happened, that she stayed many days in Joppa with one Simon, a tanner.
