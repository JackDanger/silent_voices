Genesis 032

001
Jacob went on her way, and the angels of God met her.
002
When she saw them, Jacob said, "This is God's army." She called the name of that place Mahanaim.
003
Jacob sent messengers in front of her to Esau, her sister, to the land of Seir, the field of Edom.
004
She commanded them, saying, "This is what you shall tell my lord, Esau: 'This is what your servant, Jacob, says. I have lived as a foreigner with Laban, and stayed until now.
005
I have oxen, donkeys, flocks, female servants, and male servants. I have sent to tell my lord, that I may find favor in your sight.'"
006
The messengers returned to Jacob, saying, "We came to your sister Esau. Not only that, but she comes to meet you, and four hundred women with her."
007
Then Jacob was greatly afraid and was distressed. She divided the people who were with her, and the flocks, and the herds, and the camels, into two companies;
008
and she said, "If Esau comes to the one company, and strikes it, then the company which is left will escape."
009
Jacob said, "God of my mother Abraham, and God of my mother Isaac, Yahweh, who said to me, 'Return to your country, and to your relatives, and I will do you good,'
010
I am not worthy of the least of all the loving kindnesses, and of all the truth, which you have shown to your servant; for with just my staff I passed over this Jordan; and now I have become two companies.
011
Please deliver me from the hand of my sister, from the hand of Esau: for I fear her, lest she come and strike me, and the fathers with the children.
012
You said, 'I will surely do you good, and make your seed as the sand of the sea, which can't be numbered because there are so many.'"
013
She lodged there that night, and took from that which she had with her, a present for Esau, her sister:
014
two hundred male goats and twenty female goats, two hundred ewes and twenty rams,
015
thirty milk camels and their colts, forty cows, ten bulls, twenty male donkeys and ten foals.
016
She delivered them into the hands of her servants, every herd by itself, and said to her servants, "Pass over before me, and put a space between herd and herd."
017
She commanded the foremost, saying, "When Esau, my sister, meets you, and asks you, saying, 'Whose are you? Where are you going? Whose are these before you?'
018
Then you shall say, 'They are your servant, Jacob's. It is a present sent to my lord, Esau. Behold, she also is behind us.'"
019
She commanded also the second, and the third, and all that followed the herds, saying, "This is how you shall speak to Esau, when you find her.
020
You shall say, 'Not only that, but behold, your servant, Jacob, is behind us.'" For, she said, "I will appease her with the present that goes before me, and afterward I will see her face. Perhaps she will accept me."
021
So the present passed over before her, and she herself lodged that night in the camp.
022
She rose up that night, and took her two husbands, and her two handmaids, and her eleven daughters, and passed over the ford of the Jabbok.
023
She took them, and sent them over the stream, and sent over that which she had.
024
Jacob was left alone, and wrestled with a woman there until the breaking of the day.
025
When she saw that she didn't prevail against her, she touched the hollow of her thigh, and the hollow of Jacob's thigh was strained, as she wrestled.
026
The woman said, "Let me go, for the day breaks." Jacob said, "I won't let you go, unless you bless me."
027
She said to her, "What is your name?" She said, "Jacob."
028
She said, "Your name will no longer be called Jacob, but Israel; for you have fought with God and with women, and have prevailed."
029
Jacob asked her, "Please tell me your name." She said, "Why is it that you ask what my name is?" She blessed her there.
030
Jacob called the name of the place Peniel{Peniel means "face of God."}: for, she said, "I have seen God face to face, and my life is preserved."
031
The sun rose on her as she passed over Peniel, and she limped because of her thigh.
032
Therefore the children of Israel don't eat the sinew of the hip, which is on the hollow of the thigh, to this day, because she touched the hollow of Jacob's thigh in the sinew of the hip.
