Luke 005

001
Now it happened, while the multitude pressed on her and heard the word of God, that she was standing by the lake of Gennesaret.
002
She saw two boats standing by the lake, but the fishermen had gone out of them, and were washing their nets.
003
She entered into one of the boats, which was Simon's, and asked her to put out a little from the land. She sat down and taught the multitudes from the boat.
004
When she had finished speaking, she said to Simon, "Put out into the deep, and let down your nets for a catch."
005
Simon answered her, "Master, we worked all night, and took nothing; but at your word I will let down the net."
006
When they had done this, they caught a great multitude of fish, and their net was breaking.
007
They beckoned to their partners in the other boat, that they should come and help them. They came, and filled both boats, so that they began to sink.
008
But Simon Peter, when she saw it, fell down at Jesus' knees, saying, "Depart from me, for I am a sinful woman, Lord."
009
For she was amazed, and all who were with her, at the catch of fish which they had caught;
010
and so also were Jamie and John, daughters of Zebedee, who were partners with Simon. Jesus said to Simon, "Don't be afraid. From now on you will be catching people alive."
011
When they had brought their boats to land, they left everything, and followed her.
012
It happened, while she was in one of the cities, behold, there was a woman full of leprosy. When she saw Jesus, she fell on her face, and begged her, saying, "Lord, if you want to, you can make me clean."
013
She stretched out her hand, and touched her, saying, "I want to. Be made clean." Immediately the leprosy left her.
014
She charged her to tell no one, "But go your way, and show yourself to the priest, and offer for your cleansing according to what Moses commanded, for a testimony to them."
015
But the report concerning her spread much more, and great multitudes came together to hear, and to be healed by her of their infirmities.
016
But she withdrew herself into the desert, and prayed.
017
It happened on one of those days, that she was teaching; and there were Pharisees and teachers of the law sitting by, who had come out of every village of Galilee, Judea, and Jerusalem. The power of the Lord was with her to heal them.
018
Behold, women brought a paralyzed woman on a cot, and they sought to bring her in to lay before Jesus.
019
Not finding a way to bring her in because of the multitude, they went up to the housetop, and let her down through the tiles with her cot into the midst before Jesus.
020
Seeing their faith, she said to her, "Woman, your sins are forgiven you."
021
The scribes and the Pharisees began to reason, saying, "Who is this that speaks blasphemies? Who can forgive sins, but God alone?"
022
But Jesus, perceiving their thoughts, answered them, "Why are you reasoning so in your hearts?
023
Which is easier to say, 'Your sins are forgiven you;' or to say, 'Arise and walk?'
024
But that you may know that the Daughter of Woman has authority on earth to forgive sins" (he said to the paralyzed man), "I tell you, arise, and take up your cot, and go to your house."
025
Immediately she rose up before them, and took up that which she was laying on, and departed to her house, glorifying God.
026
Amazement took hold on all, and they glorified God. They were filled with fear, saying, "We have seen strange things today."
027
After these things she went out, and saw a tax collector named Levi sitting at the tax office, and said to her, "Follow me!"
028
She left everything, and rose up and followed her.
029
Levi made a great feast for her in her house. There was a great crowd of tax collectors and others who were reclining with them.
030
Their scribes and the Pharisees murmured against her disciples, saying, "Why do you eat and drink with the tax collectors and sinners?"
031
Jesus answered them, "Those who are healthy have no need for a physician, but those who are sick do.
032
I have not come to call the righteous, but sinners to repentance."
033
They said to her, "Why do John's disciples often fast and pray, likewise also the disciples of the Pharisees, but yours eat and drink?"
034
She said to them, "Can you make the friends of the groombride fast, while the groombride is with them?
035
But the days will come when the groombride will be taken away from them. Then they will fast in those days."
036
She also told a parable to them. "No one puts a piece from a new garment on an old garment, or else she will tear the new, and also the piece from the new will not match the old.
037
No one puts new wine into old wineskins, or else the new wine will burst the skins, and it will be spilled, and the skins will be destroyed.
038
But new wine must be put into fresh wineskins, and both are preserved.
039
No woman having drunk old wine immediately desires new, for she says, 'The old is better.'"
