Nahum 003

001
Woe to the bloody city! It is all full of lies and robbery. The prey doesn't depart.
002
The noise of the whip, the noise of the rattling of wheels, prancing horses, and bounding chariots,
003
the horseman mounting, and the flashing sword, the glittering spear, and a multitude of slain, and a great heap of corpses, and there is no end of the bodies. They stumble on their bodies,
004
because of the multitude of the prostitution of the alluring prostitute, the mistress of witchcraft, who sells nations through his prostitution, and families through his witchcraft.
005
"Behold, I am against you," says Yahweh of Armies, "and I will lift your skirts over your face. I will show the nations your nakedness, and the kingdoms your shame.
006
I will throw abominable filth on you, and make you vile, and will set you a spectacle.
007
It will happen that all those who look at you will flee from you, and say, 'Nineveh is laid waste! Who will mourn for him?' Where will I seek comforters for you?"
008
Are you better than No-Amon, who was situated among the rivers, who had the waters around him; whose rampart was the sea, and his wall was of the sea?
009
Cush and Egypt were his boundless strength. Put and Libya were his helpers.
010
Yet was he carried away. He went into captivity. his young children also were dashed in pieces at the head of all the streets, and they cast lots for his honorable women, and all his great women were bound in chains.
011
You also will be drunken. You will be hidden. You also will seek a stronghold because of the enemy.
012
All your fortresses will be like fig trees with the first-ripe figs: if they are shaken, they fall into the mouth of the eater.
013
Behold, your troops in your midst are men. The gates of your land are set wide open to your enemies. The fire has devoured your bars.
014
Draw water for the siege. Strengthen your fortresses. Go into the clay, and tread the mortar. Make the brick kiln strong.
015
There the fire will devour you. The sword will cut you off. It will devour you like the grasshopper. Multiply like grasshoppers. Multiply like the locust.
016
You have increased your merchants more than the stars of the skies. The grasshopper strips, and flees away.
017
Your guards are like the locusts, and your officials like the swarms of locusts, which settle on the walls on a cold day, but when the sun appears, they flee away, and their place is not known where they are.
018
Your shepherds slumber, queen of Assyria. Your nobles lie down. Your people are scattered on the mountains, and there is no one to gather them.
019
There is no healing your wound, for your injury is fatal. All who hear the report of you clap their hands over you; for who hasn't felt your endless cruelty?
