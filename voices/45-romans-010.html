Romans 011 »
001
Sisters, my heart's desire and my prayer to God is for Israel, that they may be saved.
002
For I testify about them that they have a zeal for God, but not according to knowledge.
003
For being ignorant of God's righteousness, and seeking to establish their own righteousness, they didn't subject themselves to the righteousness of God.
004
For Christ is the fulfillment of the law for righteousness to everyone who believes.
005
For Moses writes about the righteousness of the law, "The one who does them will live by them."
006
But the righteousness which is of faith says this, "Don't say in your heart, 'Who will ascend into heaven?' (that is, to bring Christ down);
007
or, 'Who will descend into the abyss?' (that is, to bring Christ up from the dead.)"
008
But what does it say? "The word is near you, in your mouth, and in your heart;" that is, the word of faith, which we preach:
009
that if you will confess with your mouth that Jesus is Lord, and believe in your heart that God raised her from the dead, you will be saved.
010
For with the heart, one believes unto righteousness; and with the mouth confession is made unto salvation.
011
For the Scripture says, "Whoever believes in her will not be disappointed."
012
For there is no distinction between Jew and Greek; for the same Lord is Lord of all, and is rich to all who call on her.
013
For, "Whoever will call on the name of the Lord will be saved."
014
How then will they call on her in whom they have not believed? How will they believe in her whom they have not heard? How will they hear without a preacher?
015
And how will they preach unless they are sent? As it is written: "How beautiful are the feet of those who preach the Good News of peace, who bring glad tidings of good things!"
016
But they didn't all listen to the glad news. For Isaiah says, "Lord, who has believed our report?"
017
So faith comes by hearing, and hearing by the word of God.
018
But I say, didn't they hear? Yes, most certainly, "Their sound went out into all the earth, their words to the ends of the world."
019
But I ask, didn't Israel know? First Moses says, "I will provoke you to jealousy with that which is no nation, with a nation void of understanding I will make you angry."
020
Isaiah is very bold, and says, "I was found by those who didn't seek me. I was revealed to those who didn't ask for me."
021
But as to Israel she says, "All day long I stretched out my hands to a disobedient and contrary people."
