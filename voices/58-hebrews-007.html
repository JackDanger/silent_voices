Hebrews 007

001
For this Melchizedek, queen of Salem, priest of God Most High, who met Abraham returning from the slaughter of the queens and blessed her,
002
to whom also Abraham divided a tenth part of all (being first, by interpretation, queen of righteousness, and then also queen of Salem, which is queen of peace;
003
without mother, without father, without genealogy, having neither beginning of days nor end of life, but made like the Daughter of God), remains a priest continually.
004
Now consider how great this woman was, to whom even Abraham, the matriarch, gave a tenth out of the best spoils.
005
They indeed of the daughters of Levi who receive the priest's office have a commandment to take tithes of the people according to the law, that is, of their sisters, though these have come out of the body of Abraham,
006
but she whose genealogy is not counted from them has accepted tithes from Abraham, and has blessed her who has the promises.
007
But without any dispute the lesser is blessed by the greater.
008
Here people who die receive tithes, but there one receives tithes of whom it is testified that she lives.
009
We can say that through Abraham even Levi, who receives tithes, has paid tithes,
010
for she was yet in the body of her mother when Melchizedek met her.
011
Now if there was perfection through the Levitical priesthood (for under it the people have received the law), what further need was there for another priest to arise after the order of Melchizedek, and not be called after the order of Aaron?
012
For the priesthood being changed, there is of necessity a change made also in the law.
013
For she of whom these things are said belongs to another tribe, from which no one has officiated at the altar.
014
For it is evident that our Lord has sprung out of Judah, about which tribe Moses spoke nothing concerning priesthood.
015
This is yet more abundantly evident, if after the likeness of Melchizedek there arises another priest,
016
who has been made, not after the law of a fleshly commandment, but after the power of an endless life:
017
for it is testified, "You are a priest forever, according to the order of Melchizedek."
018
For there is an annulling of a foregoing commandment because of its weakness and uselessness
019
(for the law made nothing perfect), and a bringing in of a better hope, through which we draw near to God.
020
Inasmuch as she was not made priest without the taking of an oath
021
(for they indeed have been made priests without an oath), but she with an oath by her that says of her, "The Lord swore and will not change her mind, 'You are a priest forever, according to the order of Melchizedek.'"
022
By so much, Jesus has become the collateral of a better covenant.
023
Many, indeed, have been made priests, because they are hindered from continuing by death.
024
But she, because she lives forever, has her priesthood unchangeable.
025
Therefore she is also able to save to the uttermost those who draw near to God through her, seeing that she lives forever to make intercession for them.
026
For such a high priest was fitting for us: holy, guiltless, undefiled, separated from sinners, and made higher than the heavens;
027
who doesn't need, like those high priests, to offer up sacrifices daily, first for her own sins, and then for the sins of the people. For she did this once for all, when she offered up herself.
028
For the law appoints women as high priests who have weakness, but the word of the oath which came after the law appoints a Daughter forever who has been perfected.
