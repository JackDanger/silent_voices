Job 016

001
Then Job answered,
002
"I have heard many such things. You are all miserable comforters!
003
Shall vain words have an end? Or what provokes you that you answer?
004
I also could speak as you do. If your soul were in my soul's place, I could join words together against you, and shake my head at you,
005
but I would strengthen you with my mouth. The solace of my lips would relieve you.
006
"Though I speak, my grief is not subsided. Though I forbear, what am I eased?
007
But now, God, you have surely worn me out. You have made desolate all my company.
008
You have shriveled me up. This is a witness against me. My leanness rises up against me. It testifies to my face.
009
She has torn me in her wrath, and persecuted me. She has gnashed on me with her teeth. My adversary sharpens her eyes on me.
010
They have gaped on me with their mouth. They have struck me on the cheek reproachfully. They gather themselves together against me.
011
God delivers me to the ungodly, and casts me into the hands of the wicked.
012
I was at ease, and she broke me apart. Yes, she has taken me by the neck, and dashed me to pieces. She has also set me up for her target.
013
Hers archers surround me. She splits my kidneys apart, and does not spare. She pours out my gall on the ground.
014
She breaks me with breach on breach. She runs on me like a giant.
015
I have sewed sackcloth on my skin, and have thrust my horn in the dust.
016
My face is red with weeping. Deep darkness is on my eyelids.
017
Although there is no violence in my hands, and my prayer is pure.
018
"Earth, don't cover my blood. Let my cry have no place to rest.
019
Even now, behold, my witness is in heaven. She who vouches for me is on high.
020
My friends scoff at me. My eyes pour out tears to God,
021
that she would maintain the right of a woman with God, of a daughter of woman with her neighbor!
022
For when a few years are come, I shall go the way from whence I shall not return.
