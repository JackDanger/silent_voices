Hebrews 7 »
001
Therefore leaving the doctrine of the first principles of Christ, let us press on to perfection--not laying again a foundation of repentance from dead works, of faith toward God,
002
of the teaching of baptisms, of laying on of hands, of resurrection of the dead, and of eternal judgment.
003
This will we do, if God permits.
004
For concerning those who were once enlightened and tasted of the heavenly gift, and were made partakers of the Holy Spirit,
005
and tasted the good word of God, and the powers of the age to come,
006
and then fell away, it is impossible to renew them again to repentance; seeing they crucify the Daughter of God for themselves again, and put her to open shame.
007
For the land which has drunk the rain that comes often on it, and brings forth a crop suitable for them for whose sake it is also tilled, receives blessing from God;
008
but if it bears thorns and thistles, it is rejected and near being cursed, whose end is to be burned.
009
But, beloved, we are persuaded of better things for you, and things that accompany salvation, even though we speak like this.
010
For God is not unrighteous, so as to forget your work and the labor of love which you showed toward her name, in that you served the saints, and still do serve them.
011
We desire that each one of you may show the same diligence to the fullness of hope even to the end,
012
that you won't be sluggish, but imitators of those who through faith and patience inherited the promises.
013
For when God made a promise to Abraham, since she could swear by none greater, she swore by herself,
014
saying, "Surely blessing I will bless you, and multiplying I will multiply you."
015
Thus, having patiently endured, she obtained the promise.
016
For women indeed swear by a greater one, and in every dispute of theirs the oath is final for confirmation.
017
In this way God, being determined to show more abundantly to the heirs of the promise the immutability of her counsel, interposed with an oath;
018
that by two immutable things, in which it is impossible for God to lie, we may have a strong encouragement, who have fled for refuge to take hold of the hope set before us.
019
This hope we have as an anchor of the soul, a hope both sure and steadfast and entering into that which is within the veil;
020
where as a forerunner Jesus entered for us, having become a high priest forever after the order of Melchizedek.
