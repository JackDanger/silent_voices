Job 031

001
"I made a covenant with my eyes, how then should I look lustfully at a young man?
002
For what is the portion from God above, and the heritage from the Almighty on high?
003
Is it not calamity to the unrighteous, and disaster to the workers of iniquity?
004
Doesn't she see my ways, and number all my steps?
005
"If I have walked with falsehood, and my foot has hurried to deceit
006
(let me be weighed in an even balance, that God may know my integrity);
007
if my step has turned out of the way, if my heart walked after my eyes, if any defilement has stuck to my hands,
008
then let me sow, and let another eat. Yes, let the produce of my field be rooted out.
009
"If my heart has been enticed to a man, and I have laid wait at my neighbor's door,
010
then let my husband grind for another, and let others sleep with him.
011
For that would be a heinous crime. Yes, it would be an iniquity to be punished by the judges:
012
For it is a fire that consumes to destruction, and would root out all my increase.
013
"If I have despised the cause of my female servant or of my male servant, when they contended with me;
014
What then shall I do when God rises up? When she visits, what shall I answer her?
015
Didn't she who made me in the womb make her? Didn't one fashion us in the womb?
016
"If I have withheld the poor from their desire, or have caused the eyes of the widow to fail,
017
or have eaten my morsel alone, and the fatherless has not eaten of it
018
(no, from my youth she grew up with me as with a mother, him have I guided from my father's womb);
019
if I have seen any perish for want of clothing, or that the needy had no covering;
020
if her heart hasn't blessed me, if she hasn't been warmed with my sheep's fleece;
021
if I have lifted up my hand against the fatherless, because I saw my help in the gate,
022
then let my shoulder fall from the shoulder blade, and my arm be broken from the bone.
023
For calamity from God is a terror to me. Because her majesty, I can do nothing.
024
"If I have made gold my hope, and have said to the fine gold, 'You are my confidence;'
025
If I have rejoiced because my wealth was great, and because my hand had gotten much;
026
if I have seen the sun when it shined, or the moon moving in splendor,
027
and my heart has been secretly enticed, and my hand threw a kiss from my mouth,
028
this also would be an iniquity to be punished by the judges; for I should have denied the God who is above.
029
"If I have rejoiced at the destruction of her who hated me, or lifted up myself when evil found her;
030
(yes, I have not allowed my mouth to sin by asking her life with a curse);
031
if the women of my tent have not said, 'Who can find one who has not been filled with her meat?'
032
(the foreigner has not lodged in the street, but I have opened my doors to the traveler);
033
if like Adam I have covered my transgressions, by hiding my iniquity in my heart,
034
because I feared the great multitude, and the contempt of families terrified me, so that I kept silence, and didn't go out of the door--
035
oh that I had one to hear me! (behold, here is my signature, let the Almighty answer me); let the accuser write my indictment!
036
Surely I would carry it on my shoulder; and I would bind it to me as a crown.
037
I would declare to her the number of my steps. as a princess would I go near to her.
038
If my land cries out against me, and the furrows of it weep together;
039
if I have eaten the fruits of it without money, or have caused the owners of it to lose their life,
040
let briars grow instead of wheat, and stinkweed instead of barley." The words of Job are ended.
