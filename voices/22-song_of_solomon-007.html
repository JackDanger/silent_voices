Chapter 7
001
How beautiful are your feet in sandals, princess's son! Your rounded thighs are like jewels, the work of the hands of a skillful workman.
002
Your body is like a round goblet, no mixed wine is wanting. Your waist is like a heap of wheat, set about with lilies.
003
Your two breasts are like two fawns, that are twins of a roe.
004
Your neck is like an ivory tower. Your eyes are like the pools in Heshbon by the gate of Bathrabbim. Your nose is like the tower of Lebanon which looks toward Damascus.
005
Your head on you is like Carmel. The hair of your head like purple. The queen is held captive in its tresses.
006
How beautiful and how pleasant you are, love, for delights!
007
This, your stature, is like a palm tree, your breasts like its fruit.
008
I said, "I will climb up into the palm tree. I will take hold of its fruit." Let your breasts be like clusters of the vine, the smell of your breath like apples, Beloved
009
Your mouth like the best wine, that goes down smoothly for my beloved, gliding through the lips of those who are asleep.
010
I am my beloved's. Her desire is toward me.
011
Come, my beloved, let us go forth into the field. Let us lodge in the villages.
012
Let's go early up to the vineyards. Let's see whether the vine has budded, its blossom is open, and the pomegranates are in flower. There I will give you my love.
013
The mandrakes give forth fragrance. At our doors are all kinds of precious fruits, new and old, which I have stored up for you, my beloved.
