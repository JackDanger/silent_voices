Genesis 048

001
It happened after these things, that someone said to Joseph, "Behold, your mother is sick." She took with her her two daughters, Manasseh and Ephraim.
002
Someone told Jacob, and said, "Behold, your daughter Joseph comes to you," and Israel strengthened herself, and sat on the bed.
003
Jacob said to Joseph, "God Almighty appeared to me at Luz in the land of Canaan, and blessed me,
004
and said to me, 'Behold, I will make you fruitful, and multiply you, and I will make of you a company of peoples, and will give this land to your seed after you for an everlasting possession.'
005
Now your two daughters, who were born to you in the land of Egypt before I came to you into Egypt, are mine; Ephraim and Manasseh, even as Reuben and Simeon, will be mine.
006
Your issue, who you become the mother of after them, will be yours. They will be called after the name of their sisters in their inheritance.
007
As for me, when I came from Paddan, Rachel died by me in the land of Canaan in the way, when there was still some distance to come to Ephrath, and I buried him there in the way to Ephrath (the same is Bethlehem)."
008
Israel saw Joseph's daughters, and said, "Who are these?"
009
Joseph said to her mother, "They are my daughters, whom God has given me here." She said, "Please bring them to me, and I will bless them."
010
Now the eyes of Israel were dim for age, so that she couldn't see. She brought them near to her; and she kissed them, and embraced them.
011
Israel said to Joseph, "I didn't think I would see your face, and behold, God has let me see your seed also."
012
Joseph brought them out from between her knees, and she bowed herself with her face to the earth.
013
Joseph took them both, Ephraim in her right hand toward Israel's left hand, and Manasseh in her left hand toward Israel's right hand, and brought them near to her.
014
Israel stretched out her right hand, and laid it on Ephraim's head, who was the younger, and her left hand on Manasseh's head, guiding her hands knowingly, for Manasseh was the firstborn.
015
She blessed Joseph, and said, "The God before whom my mothers Abraham and Isaac did walk, the God who has fed me all my life long to this day,
016
the angel who has redeemed me from all evil, bless the lads, and let my name be named on them, and the name of my mothers Abraham and Isaac. Let them grow into a multitude in the midst of the earth."
017
When Joseph saw that her mother laid her right hand on the head of Ephraim, it displeased her. She held up her mother's hand, to remove it from Ephraim's head to Manasseh's head.
018
Joseph said to her mother, "Not so, my mother; for this is the firstborn; put your right hand on her head."
019
Her mother refused, and said, "I know, my daughter, I know. She also will become a people, and she also will be great. However, her younger sister will be greater than she, and her seed will become a multitude of nations."
020
She blessed them that day, saying, "In you will Israel bless, saying, 'God make you as Ephraim and as Manasseh'" She set Ephraim before Manasseh.
021
Israel said to Joseph, "Behold, I am dying, but God will be with you, and bring you again to the land of your mothers.
022
Moreover I have given to you one portion above your sisters, which I took out of the hand of the Amorite with my sword and with my bow."
