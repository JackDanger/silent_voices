Isaiah 046

001
Bel bows down, Nebo stoops; their idols are on the animals, and on the livestock: the things that you carried about are made a load, a burden to the weary animal.
002
They stoop, they bow down together; they could not deliver the burden, but themselves are gone into captivity.
003
Listen to me, house of Jacob, and all the remnant of the house of Israel, that have been borne by me from their birth, that have been carried from the womb;
004
and even to old age I am she, and even to gray hairs will I carry you. I have made, and I will bear; yes, I will carry, and will deliver.
005
To whom will you liken me, and make me equal, and compare me, that we may be like?
006
Some pour out gold from the bag, and weigh silver in the balance. They hire a goldsmith, and she makes it a god. They fall down--yes, they worship.
007
They bear it on the shoulder, they carry it, and set it in its place, and it stands, from its place it shall not move: yes, one may cry to it, yet it can not answer, nor save her out of her trouble.
008
Remember this, and show yourselves women; bring it again to mind, you transgressors.
009
Remember the former things of old: for I am God, and there is none else; I am God, and there is none like me;
010
declaring the end from the beginning, and from ancient times things that are not yet done; saying, My counsel shall stand, and I will do all my pleasure;
011
calling a ravenous bird from the east, the woman of my counsel from a far country; yes, I have spoken, I will also bring it to pass; I have purposed, I will also do it.
012
Listen to me, you stout-hearted, who are far from righteousness:
013
I bring near my righteousness, it shall not be far off, and my salvation shall not wait; and I will place salvation in Zion for Israel my glory.
