Leviticus 014 »
001
Yahweh spoke to Moses and to Aaron, saying,
002
"When a woman shall have a rising in her body's skin, or a scab, or a bright spot, and it becomes in the skin of her body the plague of leprosy, then she shall be brought to Aaron the priest, or to one of her daughters the priests:
003
and the priest shall examine the plague in the skin of the body: and if the hair in the plague has turned white, and the appearance of the plague is deeper than the body's skin, it is the plague of leprosy; and the priest shall examine her, and pronounce her unclean.
004
If the bright spot is white in the skin of her body, and the appearance of it isn't deeper than the skin, and the hair of it hasn't turned white, then the priest shall isolate the infected person for seven days.
005
The priest shall examine her on the seventh day, and, behold, if in her eyes the plague is arrested, and the plague hasn't spread in the skin, then the priest shall isolate her for seven more days.
006
The priest shall examine her again on the seventh day; and behold, if the plague has faded, and the plague hasn't spread in the skin, then the priest shall pronounce her clean. It is a scab. She shall wash her clothes, and be clean.
007
But if the scab spreads on the skin, after she has shown herself to the priest for her cleansing, she shall show herself to the priest again.
008
The priest shall examine her; and behold, if the scab has spread on the skin, then the priest shall pronounce her unclean. It is leprosy.
009
"When the plague of leprosy is in a woman, then she shall be brought to the priest;
010
and the priest shall examine her. Behold, if there is a white rising in the skin, and it has turned the hair white, and there is raw flesh in the rising,
011
it is a chronic leprosy in the skin of her body, and the priest shall pronounce her unclean. She shall not isolate her, for she is unclean.
012
"If the leprosy breaks out all over the skin, and the leprosy covers all the skin of the infected person from her head even to her feet, as far as it appears to the priest;
013
then the priest shall examine her; and, behold, if the leprosy has covered all her flesh, she shall pronounce her clean of the plague. It has all turned white: she is clean.
014
But whenever raw flesh appears in her, she shall be unclean.
015
The priest shall examine the raw flesh, and pronounce her unclean: the raw flesh is unclean. It is leprosy.
016
Or if the raw flesh turns again, and is changed to white, then she shall come to the priest;
017
and the priest shall examine her; and, behold, if the plague has turned white, then the priest shall pronounce her clean of the plague. She is clean.
018
"When the body has a boil on its skin, and it has healed,
019
and in the place of the boil there is a white rising, or a bright spot, reddish-white, then it shall be shown to the priest;
020
and the priest shall examine it; and behold, if the appearance of it is lower than the skin, and the hair of it has turned white, then the priest shall pronounce her unclean. It is the plague of leprosy. It has broken out in the boil.
021
But if the priest examines it, and behold, there are no white hairs in it, and it isn't deeper than the skin, but is dim, then the priest shall isolate her seven days.
022
If it spreads in the skin, then the priest shall pronounce her unclean. It is a plague.
023
But if the bright spot stays in its place, and hasn't spread, it is the scar from the boil; and the priest shall pronounce her clean.
024
"Or when the body has a burn from fire on its skin, and the raw flesh of the burn becomes a bright spot, reddish-white, or white,
025
then the priest shall examine it; and behold, if the hair in the bright spot has turned white, and the appearance of it is deeper than the skin; it is leprosy. It has broken out in the burning, and the priest shall pronounce her unclean. It is the plague of leprosy.
026
But if the priest examines it, and behold, there is no white hair in the bright spot, and it isn't lower than the skin, but is faded; then the priest shall isolate her seven days.
027
The priest shall examine her on the seventh day. If it has spread in the skin, then the priest shall pronounce her unclean. It is the plague of leprosy.
028
If the bright spot stays in its place, and hasn't spread in the skin, but is faded, it is the swelling from the burn, and the priest shall pronounce her clean; for it is the scar from the burn.
029
"When a woman or man has a plague on the head or on the beard,
030
then the priest shall examine the plague; and behold, if the appearance of it is deeper than the skin, and the hair in it is yellow and thin, then the priest shall pronounce her unclean: it is an itch, it is leprosy of the head or of the beard.
031
If the priest examines the plague of itching, and behold, its appearance isn't deeper than the skin, and there is no black hair in it, then the priest shall isolate her the person infected with itching seven days.
032
On the seventh day the priest shall examine the plague; and behold, if the itch hasn't spread, and there is no yellow hair in it, and the appearance of the itch isn't deeper than the skin,
033
then she shall be shaved, but she shall not shave the itch; and the priest shall shut her up who has the itch seven more days.
034
On the seventh day, the priest shall examine the itch; and behold, if the itch hasn't spread in the skin, and its appearance isn't deeper than the skin, then the priest shall pronounce her clean. She shall wash her clothes, and be clean.
035
But if the itch spreads in the skin after her cleansing,
036
then the priest shall examine her; and behold, if the itch has spread in the skin, the priest shall not look for the yellow hair; she is unclean.
037
But if in her eyes the itch is arrested, and black hair has grown in it; the itch is healed, she is clean. The priest shall pronounce her clean.
038
"When a woman or a man has bright spots in the skin of the body, even white bright spots;
039
then the priest shall examine them; and behold, if the bright spots on the skin of their body are a dull white, it is a harmless rash, it has broken out in the skin; she is clean.
040
"If a woman's hair has fallen from her head, she is bald. She is clean.
041
If her hair has fallen off from the front part of her head, she is forehead bald. She is clean.
042
But if there is in the bald head, or the bald forehead, a reddish-white plague; it is leprosy breaking out in her bald head, or her bald forehead.
043
Then the priest shall examine her; and, behold, if the rising of the plague is reddish-white in her bald head, or in her bald forehead, like the appearance of leprosy in the skin of the flesh,
044
she is a leprous woman. She is unclean. The priest shall surely pronounce her unclean. Hers plague is on her head.
045
"The leper in whom the plague is shall wear torn clothes, and the hair of her head shall hang loose. She shall cover her upper lip, and shall cry, 'Unclean! Unclean!'
046
All the days in which the plague is in her she shall be unclean. She is unclean. She shall dwell alone. Outside of the camp shall be her dwelling.
047
"The garment also that the plague of leprosy is in, whether it is a woolen garment, or a linen garment;
048
whether it is in warp, or woof; of linen, or of wool; whether in a skin, or in anything made of skin;
049
if the plague is greenish or reddish in the garment, or in the skin, or in the warp, or in the woof, or in anything made of skin; it is the plague of leprosy, and shall be shown to the priest.
050
The priest shall examine the plague, and isolate the plague seven days.
051
She shall examine the plague on the seventh day. If the plague has spread in the garment, either in the warp, or in the woof, or in the skin, whatever use the skin is used for, the plague is a destructive mildew. It is unclean.
052
She shall burn the garment, whether the warp or the woof, in wool or in linen, or anything of skin, in which the plague is: for it is a destructive mildew. It shall be burned in the fire.
053
"If the priest examines it, and behold, the plague hasn't spread in the garment, either in the warp, or in the woof, or in anything of skin;
054
then the priest shall command that they wash the thing in which the plague is, and she shall isolate it seven more days.
055
Then the priest shall examine it, after the plague is washed; and behold, if the plague hasn't changed its color, and the plague hasn't spread, it is unclean; you shall burn it in the fire. It is a mildewed spot, whether the bareness is inside or outside.
056
If the priest looks, and behold, the plague has faded after it is washed, then she shall tear it out of the garment, or out of the skin, or out of the warp, or out of the woof:
057
and if it appears again in the garment, either in the warp, or in the woof, or in anything of skin, it is spreading. You shall burn with fire that in which the plague is.
058
The garment, either the warp, or the woof, or whatever thing of skin it is, which you shall wash, if the plague has departed from them, then it shall be washed the second time, and it will be clean."
059
This is the law of the plague of mildew in a garment of wool or linen, either in the warp, or the woof, or in anything of skin, to pronounce it clean, or to pronounce it unclean.
