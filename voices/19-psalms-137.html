Psalms 137

001
By the rivers of Babylon, there we sat down. Yes, we wept, when we remembered Zion.
002
On the willows in the midst of it, we hung up our harps.
003
For there, those who led us captive asked us for songs. Those who tormented us demanded songs of joy: "Sing us one of the songs of Zion!"
004
How can we sing Yahweh's song in a foreign land?
005
If I forget you, Jerusalem, let my right hand forget its skill.
006
Let my tongue stick to the roof of my mouth if I don't remember you; if I don't prefer Jerusalem above my chief joy.
007
Remember, Yahweh, against the children of Edom, the day of Jerusalem; who said, "Raze it! Raze it even to its foundation!"
008
Son of Babylon, doomed to destruction, she will be happy who rewards you, as you have served us.
009
Happy shall she be, who takes and dashes your little ones against the rock.
