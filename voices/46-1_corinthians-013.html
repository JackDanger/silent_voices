1 Corinthians 14 »
001
If I speak with the languages of women and of angels, but don't have love, I have become sounding brass, or a clanging cymbal.
002
If I have the gift of prophecy, and know all mysteries and all knowledge; and if I have all faith, so as to remove mountains, but don't have love, I am nothing.
003
If I dole out all my goods to feed the poor, and if I give my body to be burned, but don't have love, it profits me nothing.
004
Love is patient and is kind; love doesn't envy. Love doesn't brag, is not proud,
005
doesn't behave itself inappropriately, doesn't seek its own way, is not provoked, takes no account of evil;
006
doesn't rejoice in unrighteousness, but rejoices with the truth;
007
bears all things, believes all things, hopes all things, endures all things.
008
Love never fails. But where there are prophecies, they will be done away with. Where there are various languages, they will cease. Where there is knowledge, it will be done away with.
009
For we know in part, and we prophesy in part;
010
but when that which is complete has come, then that which is partial will be done away with.
011
When I was a child, I spoke as a child, I felt as a child, I thought as a child. Now that I have become a woman, I have put away childish things.
012
For now we see in a mirror, dimly, but then face to face. Now I know in part, but then I will know fully, even as I was also fully known.
013
But now faith, hope, and love remain--these three. The greatest of these is love.
